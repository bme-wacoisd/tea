# Slides: Heather Cox Richardson — Teaching American History

## Planning Notes

This lesson teaches future educators how to make history relevant by following Richardson's approach:
- Start with stories, not generalizations
- Connect past to present
- Trust students with facts
- Be approachable, not intimidating

## Slide Flow

1. **Title**: Heather Cox Richardson - Boston College, Letters from an American
2. **Who Is She?**: Professor, PhD Harvard, 2.6M newsletter subscribers
3. **Quote**: "The past doesn't repeat itself. It rhymes."
4. **Big Idea**: If people have true facts, they make good decisions
5. **Quote**: "I am a teacher at heart"
6. **Her Method**: Start with story, not abstraction
7. **Connect Past to Present**: Table showing historical topics → current relevance
8. **Teaching History IS/IS NOT**: Comparison slide
9. **Harkness Method**: Discussion-based learning
10. **Adapting for Ages**: Table by grade level
11. **Group Discussion**: Instructions
12. **Closing Quote**: "To understand the present, we have to understand how we got here"
