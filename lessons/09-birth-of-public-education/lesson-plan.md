# Lesson Plan: The Birth of Public Education

**Course**: Grow Your Own Educator Preparation Program
**Grade Level**: 9-12 (Future Educators)
**Duration**: 35-40 minutes
**Instructor**: Brian Edwards (brian.edwards@wacoisd.org)
**Date Prepared**: December 17, 2024

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
1. Explain who Horace Mann was and why he is called "The Father of American Public Education"
2. Define "common school" and identify its key characteristics
3. Describe Mann's six propositions about education
4. Connect Mann's historical reforms to features of modern schools

## Lesson Sequence

### Opening (5 minutes)
- Display: "The year is 1840. Most American children don't go to school."
- Ask: What would it be like if only wealthy kids could attend school? If your teacher was a teenager with no training?
- Brief discussion to establish historical context

### Direct Instruction (10 minutes)
- Present slides on Mann's background: poverty, limited education, rise to leadership
- Explain "common school" concept and its four characteristics
- Compare before/after Mann (table slide)
- Discuss normal schools and professional teaching

### Read-Aloud (5 minutes)
- Read the reading.md passage aloud while students follow along
- Emphasize the radical nature of Mann's ideas for his time

### Group Discussion (12-15 minutes)
- Divide into small groups (3-4 students)
- Distribute worksheet with discussion prompts
- Students discuss prompts verbally
- Instructor circulates to facilitate deeper thinking

### Whole Class Debrief (5 minutes)
- Groups share key insights
- Address ongoing debates: Who should control schools? What is the purpose of public education?
- Connect to students' future roles as public school teachers

### Closing (3 minutes)
- Return to Mann's final words: "Be ashamed to die until you have won some victory for humanity."
- Challenge: The schools you will teach in exist because of Mann's vision. What will your contribution be?

---

## Assessment Strategy

- **Formative**: Observation during group discussion; quality of historical connections
- **Summative**: Quiz on key concepts (Google Forms)

---

## Differentiation Notes

- **For struggling readers**: Reading passage uses accessible language; consider partner reading
- **For advanced students**: Research Mann's opponents and their arguments
- **For ELL students**: Pre-teach vocabulary (common school, normal school, compulsory, republic)
