# Lesson Plan: Teaching in the Zone

**Course**: Grow Your Own Educator Preparation Program
**Grade Level**: 9-12 (Future Educators)
**Duration**: 35-40 minutes
**Instructor**: Brian Edwards (brian.edwards@wacoisd.org)
**Date Prepared**: December 17, 2024

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
1. Explain the Zone of Proximal Development and the three zones of learning
2. Define scaffolding and describe its key characteristics
3. Recognize signs that students are in, above, or below their zone
4. Apply the "I Do, We Do, You Do" framework to instructional planning

## Lesson Sequence

### Opening (5 minutes)
- Ask: "Think about learning to ride a bike. What kind of help did you need at first? How did that help change over time?"
- Brief discussion connecting to the concept of scaffolding
- Introduce the video game analogy: Level 1 is challenging but achievable

### Direct Instruction (10 minutes)
- Present the three zones (comfort, ZPD, frustration)
- Introduce Vygotsky and the Zone of Proximal Development
- Explain scaffolding with training wheels metaphor
- Show the "I Do, We Do, You Do" structure

### Read-Aloud (5 minutes)
- Read the reading.md passage aloud while students follow along
- Pause to reinforce key concepts (scaffolding, fading, independence)

### Group Discussion (12-15 minutes)
- Divide into small groups (3-4 students)
- Distribute worksheet with discussion prompts
- Students discuss prompts verbally
- Instructor circulates to facilitate and probe deeper thinking

### Whole Class Debrief (5 minutes)
- Groups share key insights
- Address: How do you find the zone for different students?
- Discuss the difference between helpful scaffolding and doing the work for students

## Assessment Strategy

- **Formative**: Observation during group discussion; application to real scenarios
- **Summative**: Quiz on key concepts (Google Forms)

---

## Differentiation Notes

- **For struggling readers**: Reading passage uses concrete examples; partner reading option
- **For advanced students**: Compare Vygotsky's social view with Piaget's individual view
- **For ELL students**: Pre-teach vocabulary (scaffold, zone, proximal, fading)

---

## Instructor Notes

This lesson should MODEL scaffolded instruction. Start with accessible examples (bike, video games), build to formal concepts, gradually release to student discussion.

The "I Do, We Do, You Do" structure can be demonstrated within this very lesson.
