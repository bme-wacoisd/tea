# Group Discussion: Teaching the 4 Cs

## Prompt 1: Content Knowledge Debate
Why do some argue that content knowledge is less important than it used to be? Do you agree? What's the role of facts in a world where anything can be looked up?

## Prompt 2: Embedding the 4 Cs
Pick one of the 4 Cs (Critical Thinking, Collaboration, Communication, Creativity). How would you teach it in a math lesson? A history lesson?

## Prompt 3: Real Collaboration
What's the difference between putting students in groups and teaching collaboration? What makes group work actually develop collaboration skills?

## Prompt 4: Assessing Creativity
How might you assess creativity fairly? What would you look for? What would a rubric for creativity include?

## Prompt 5: AI and the 4 Cs
AI can now write essays, create images, and answer questions. Which of the 4 Cs does this make MORE important, not less? Why?

## Prompt 6: Jobs That Don't Exist Yet
Think of a job that didn't exist 20 years ago. What skills does it require? What skills might jobs require in 20 more years?
