# Lesson Plan: Following the Child

**Course**: Grow Your Own Educator Preparation Program
**Grade Level**: 9-12 (Future Educators)
**Duration**: 35-40 minutes
**Instructor**: Brian Edwards (brian.edwards@wacoisd.org)
**Date Prepared**: December 17, 2024

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
1. Explain Maria Montessori's approach to child-centered education
2. Describe what a "prepared environment" looks like and why it matters
3. Define "sensitive periods" and give examples
4. Contrast teacher-centered and child-centered approaches to instruction

## Lesson Sequence

### Opening (5 minutes)
- Show two classroom photos: traditional rows vs. Montessori environment
- Ask: "What differences do you notice? Which would you rather learn in?"
- Introduce Montessori and her discovery in Rome

### Direct Instruction (10 minutes)
- Present Montessori's background and the "unteachable" children
- Explain the prepared environment concept
- Introduce sensitive periods with examples
- Contrast teacher role: sage-on-stage vs. guide-on-side
- Discuss self-correcting materials and why they matter
- Share the "explosion into writing" story

### Read-Aloud (5 minutes)
- Read the reading.md passage aloud while students follow along
- Pause at "follow the child" to discuss what this really means

### Group Discussion (12-15 minutes)
- Divide into small groups (3-4 students)
- Distribute worksheet with discussion prompts
- Key discussion: How to balance following the child with required curriculum
- Instructor circulates to push thinking about practical applications

### Whole Class Debrief (5 minutes)
- Groups share insights on adapting Montessori principles
- Address the "but I have to teach standards" concern
- Discuss what observation before teaching might reveal

### Closing (3 minutes)
- Key message: Following the child means meeting them where they're ready, not abandoning structure
- Challenge: This week, observe a young child (sibling, neighbor, etc.) - what are they drawn to?

---

## Assessment Strategy

- **Formative**: Quality of discussion about balancing child-centered approaches with requirements
- **Summative**: Quiz on prepared environment, sensitive periods, and Montessori teacher role

---

## Differentiation Notes

- **For struggling students**: Provide a Venn diagram comparing traditional and Montessori classrooms
- **For advanced students**: Research public Montessori schools and their outcomes
- **For ELL students**: Pre-teach vocabulary (sensitive period, prepared environment, self-correcting)

---

## Instructor Notes

Model child-centered principles during this lesson: offer choice in discussion prompts, avoid interrupting student thinking, trust students to construct understanding.

The key tension to explore is the practical one: "How do I follow the child when I have required standards and 25 students?" Acknowledge this is genuinely hard while pointing out that even small shifts (more observation, more choice, more independence) can move toward child-centered practice.
