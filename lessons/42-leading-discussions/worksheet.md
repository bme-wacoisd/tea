# Group Discussion: Leading Discussions

## Prompt 1: Teacher Silence
What's uncomfortable about the teacher staying mostly silent during a Socratic seminar? Why is this restraint valuable?

## Prompt 2: Building on Ideas
How is building on someone's idea different from just agreeing with them? What does productive building look like?

## Prompt 3: Text as Anchor
Why is text-based discussion important? What happens when discussions don't have a shared text or evidence base?

## Prompt 4: Handling Incorrect Answers
A student says something factually incorrect during a Socratic seminar. How do you handle it without taking over the discussion?

## Prompt 5: Drawing Out Quiet Students
How do you encourage quiet students to participate without putting them on the spot or making them uncomfortable?

## Prompt 6: Teaching Discussion Skills
What skills do students need before they can have productive Socratic discussions? How would you teach these skills explicitly?
