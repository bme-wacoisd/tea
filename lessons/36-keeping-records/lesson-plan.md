# Lesson Plan: Keeping Records That Protect You and Your Students

**Course**: Grow Your Own Educator Preparation Program
**Grade Level**: 9-12 (Future Educators)
**Duration**: 35-40 minutes
**Instructor**: Brian Edwards (brian.edwards@wacoisd.org)
**Date Prepared**: December 17, 2024

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
1. Identify the types of records teachers must maintain (academic, behavioral, communication, compliance)
2. Apply best practices for documentation: timely, factual, specific, professional
3. Explain FERPA protections and confidentiality requirements for student records

## Lesson Sequence

### Opening (5 minutes)
- Present scenarios: Parent challenges grade, administrator questions intervention, colleague disputes collaboration
- Ask: "Without records, what do you have?"
- Frame: Documentation is protection, not bureaucracy

### Direct Instruction (10 minutes)
- Review types of records: academic, behavioral, communication, compliance
- Present best practices:
  - Timely: Same-day documentation
  - Factual: Events, not interpretations
  - Specific: Dates, times, details
  - Professional: Assume it could be read by anyone
- Introduce FERPA and confidentiality requirements
- Show examples of good vs. poor documentation

### Read-Aloud (5 minutes)
- Read the reading.md passage while students follow along
- Pause at the FERPA section for emphasis

### Group Discussion (12-15 minutes)
- Divide into small groups (3-4 students)
- Distribute worksheet with discussion prompts
- Key task: Convert interpretive statements to factual documentation
- Instructor circulates to guide discussions

### Whole Class Debrief (5 minutes)
- Share rewritten documentation examples
- Discuss systems for tracking parent contacts
- Address the balance between documentation and teaching

### Closing (3 minutes)
- Key message: If you think "I should probably write this down," you should
- Documentation protects students, you, and the school
- Challenge: Create a parent contact log template

---

## Assessment Strategy

- **Formative**: Quality of fact-vs-interpretation rewrites; understanding of FERPA
- **Summative**: Quiz on documentation best practices and record types

---

## Differentiation Notes

- **For struggling students**: Provide documentation templates with prompts
- **For advanced students**: Research district-specific documentation requirements
- **For ELL students**: Pre-teach vocabulary (documentation, confidentiality, compliance, FERPA)

## Instructor Notes

Students may underestimate how important documentation becomes until they need it. Use real scenarios (anonymized) where documentation saved a teacher or where lack of documentation caused problems.

The fact-vs-interpretation distinction is crucial:
- "Marcus threw his pencil" = fact
- "Marcus had an attitude" = interpretation
- "Marcus was disrespectful" = interpretation
- "Marcus said 'This is stupid'" = fact (direct quote)

FERPA violations can seem minor but have serious consequences. Even well-meaning conversations in the hallway or teacher's lounge can cross the line.
