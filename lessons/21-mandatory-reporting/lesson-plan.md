# Lesson Plan: When You Must Speak Up

**Course**: Grow Your Own Educator Preparation Program
**Grade Level**: 9-12 (Future Educators)
**Duration**: 35-40 minutes
**Instructor**: Brian Edwards (brian.edwards@wacoisd.org)
**Date Prepared**: December 17, 2024

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
1. Explain Texas mandatory reporting requirements (48-hour timeline, who must report)
2. Identify the four types of child maltreatment and their warning signs
3. Describe the proper steps for making a report
4. Distinguish between a teacher's role (reporting) and CPS's role (investigating)

## Materials Needed

- Slides presentation (Google Slides)
- Reading handout or projected text
- Worksheet for group discussion
- Quiz (Google Forms)
- Texas Abuse Hotline number displayed: 1-800-252-5400

---

## Lesson Sequence

### Opening (5 minutes)
- Ask: "If you suspected a student was being hurt at home, what would you do?"
- Collect responses without judgment
- Introduce the legal requirement: mandatory reporting

### Direct Instruction (10 minutes)
- Present the 48-hour requirement and legal consequences
- Explain the four types of maltreatment with warning signs
- Walk through how to make a report (hotline, online)
- Clarify what TO do and what NOT to do
- Discuss how to respond if a child discloses
- Address common hesitations that prevent reporting

### Read-Aloud (5 minutes)
- Read the reading.md passage aloud while students follow along
- Pause at the hesitations section for emphasis

### Group Discussion (12-15 minutes)
- Divide into small groups (3-4 students)
- Distribute worksheet with discussion prompts
- Key discussion: How to respond when a child confides in you
- Instructor circulates to ensure accurate understanding

### Whole Class Debrief (5 minutes)
- Clarify any misconceptions
- Emphasize: your job is to report, not investigate
- Address emotional weight of this responsibility

### Closing (3 minutes)
- Key message: You might be the one adult who notices and acts
- Ensure all students have the hotline number: 1-800-252-5400
- Acknowledge this is serious but they are now prepared

---

## Assessment Strategy

- **Formative**: Accuracy of understanding during discussions; ability to distinguish teacher role from CPS role
- **Summative**: Quiz on reporting timeline, warning signs, and proper procedures

---

## Differentiation Notes

- **For struggling students**: Provide a quick-reference card with the four types and key warning signs
- **For advanced students**: Research Texas Family Code requirements in detail
- **For ELL students**: Pre-teach vocabulary (mandatory, suspicion, neglect, disclosure, immunity)
