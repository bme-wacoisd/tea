## Slide 1: Title
Every Kid Needs a Champion
The Power of Believing in Students

## Slide 2: Opening Quote
"Kids don't learn from people they don't like."
— Rita Pierson

---

## Slide 3: Who Was Rita Pierson
- 40 years as a professional educator
- Parents and grandparents were educators
- Her TED Talk: over 15 million views
- Spoke about what really makes teachers effective

---

## Slide 4: The Core Insight
"No significant learning without significant relationship."
— James Comer, child psychiatrist

---

## Slide 5: George Washington Carver Quote
"All learning is understanding relationships."

---

## Slide 6: The Colleague Story
- "They don't pay me to like the kids."
- Pierson's response became legendary
- Some teachers deliver content
- Champions believe in children

---

## Slide 7: The +2 Story
A student missed 18 out of 20.
Pierson wrote "+2" with a smiley face.
"2 ain't bad!"

---

## Slide 8: Why +2 Mattered
- Same information: 2 correct answers
- Completely different message
- -18 says: "You failed"
- +2 says: "You started. Keep going."

---

## Slide 9: The Classroom Mantra
"I am somebody.
I was somebody when I came.
I'll be a better somebody when I leave.
I am powerful, and I am strong."

---

## Slide 10: The Power of Apology
- "I came in on a Monday and said..."
- "I was wrong yesterday."
- Vulnerability builds trust
- Models what we want students to learn

---

## Slide 11: Champions vs. Tolerators
Champions:
- Believe students can improve
- See potential, not just performance
- Build relationships intentionally
- Never give up on a child

Tolerators:
- Tolerate difficult students
- Focus on current behavior
- Keep professional distance
- Move struggling students along

---

## Slide 12: What AI Cannot Do
An AI can say "You can do this!"
But it doesn't believe it.
It has no stakes in your success.

---

## Slide 13: The Irreplaceable Human Element
- AI cannot genuinely believe in you
- AI cannot be vulnerable or apologize meaningfully
- AI cannot witness your growth over time
- AI cannot be your champion

---

## Slide 14: Building Relationships Table
| Strategy | What It Communicates |
|----------|---------------------|
| Learn student names quickly | "You matter as an individual" |
| Greet students at the door | "I'm glad you're here" |
| Remember details about their lives | "I see you beyond this class" |
| Celebrate small wins | "I notice your effort" |
| Admit when you're wrong | "I respect you enough to be honest" |

---

## Slide 15: Closing Quote
"Every child deserves a champion—an adult who will never give up on them, who understands the power of connection, and insists that they become the best they can possibly be."
— Rita Pierson

## Slide 16: Closing
Be the teacher who believes.
