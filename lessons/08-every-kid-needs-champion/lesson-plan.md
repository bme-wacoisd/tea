# Lesson Plan: Every Kid Needs a Champion

**Course**: Grow Your Own Educator Preparation Program
**Grade Level**: 9-12 (Future Educators)
**Duration**: 35-40 minutes
**Instructor**: Brian Edwards (brian.edwards@wacoisd.org)
**Date Prepared**: December 17, 2024

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
1. Explain why teacher-student relationships are foundational to effective learning
2. Identify specific strategies for building positive relationships with students
3. Distinguish between teachers who "champion" students and those who merely "tolerate" them
4. Articulate what human teachers provide that AI cannot replicate

## Lesson Sequence

### Opening (5 minutes)
- Display opening quote: "Kids don't learn from people they don't like."
- Ask students to silently reflect: Think of a teacher who believed in you. What made the difference?
- Brief sharing (2-3 volunteers)

### Direct Instruction (10 minutes)
- Present slides on Rita Pierson's background and philosophy
- Share the "+2" story and discuss the power of framing
- Present the "I Am Somebody" mantra
- Discuss the power of teacher vulnerability and apology

### Read-Aloud (5 minutes)
- Read the reading.md passage aloud while students follow along
- Pause after each paragraph for brief reflection

### Group Discussion (12-15 minutes)
- Divide into small groups (3-4 students)
- Distribute worksheet with discussion prompts
- Students discuss prompts verbally
- Instructor circulates to facilitate and probe deeper thinking

### Whole Class Debrief (5 minutes)
- Each group shares one key insight
- Address the AI question: What can only human teachers provide?
- Connect back to their future roles as educators

### Closing (3 minutes)
- Return to the closing quote: "Every child deserves a champion"
- Challenge: This week, notice a moment where you could be a champion for someone

---

## Assessment Strategy

- **Formative**: Observation during group discussion; quality of insights shared
- **Summative**: Quiz on key concepts (Google Forms)

---

## Differentiation Notes

- **For struggling readers**: Reading passage is written at accessible level; consider buddy reading
- **For advanced students**: Encourage connections to research on teacher-student relationships
- **For ELL students**: Pre-teach key vocabulary (champion, rapport, vulnerability)

---
