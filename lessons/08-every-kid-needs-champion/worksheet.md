## Prompt 2: The +2 Approach
Rita Pierson wrote "+2" instead of "-18" on a student's test. Both communicate the same factual information, but the message is completely different. How might you apply this "+2" thinking in different subjects or situations? What are the limits of this approach?

## Prompt 3: Champions vs. Tolerators
Pierson describes the difference between teachers who champion students and those who merely tolerate them. What does it look like, day to day, to be a champion? How might a tolerator and a champion respond differently to the same struggling student?

## Prompt 4: The Power of Apology
Pierson said, "I came in on a Monday and said, 'I was wrong yesterday.'" Why does admitting mistakes build trust? When is it appropriate for teachers to apologize to students, and when might it not be?

## Prompt 5: Professional Boundaries
How do you build genuine relationships with students while maintaining professional boundaries? Where is the line between being a champion and being a friend?

## Prompt 6: The AI Question
An AI tutor can provide unlimited patience and instant feedback at any hour. It never has a bad day. What can it NOT provide that Pierson's students received from her? As future teachers, what parts of teaching should you never outsource to technology?
