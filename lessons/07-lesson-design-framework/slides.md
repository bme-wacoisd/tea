# Slides: The Five Artifacts of Lesson Design

## Slide 1: Title
**Lesson Design**
The Five Artifacts Framework

---

## Slide 2: Big Idea - The Blueprint Metaphor
Architects draw blueprints before builders lay bricks.
Teachers design lessons before students arrive.

---

## Slide 3: The Five Artifacts Overview
1. Lesson Plan - Documentation for administrators
2. Slides - Visual support for instruction
3. Reading - Instructor read-aloud text
4. Worksheet - Group discussion prompts
5. Quiz - End-of-lesson assessment

---

---

## Slide 5: Artifact 1 - The Lesson Plan
1. Learning objectives: What students will know or do
2. Standards alignment: Which TEKS are addressed
3. Materials needed: Everything required for the lesson
4. Lesson sequence: Minute-by-minute breakdown
5. Assessment strategy: How learning is measured

---

## Slide 6: Artifact 2 - The Slides (Comparison)
**Effective Slides:**
- Key ideas, not paragraphs
- Images that support content
- Guide attention, don't overwhelm

**Ineffective Slides:**
- Walls of text to read verbatim
- Clip art that adds nothing
- Everything crammed on one slide

---

## Slide 7: Artifact 3 - The Reading
Instructor reads aloud.
Students follow along.
Listeners AND readers both get what they need.

---

## Slide 8: Artifact 4 - The Worksheet (Comparison)
**Our Worksheets:**
- Discussion prompts
- Talk with your group
- Instructor circulates and engages

**Traditional Worksheets:**
- Fill-in-the-blank
- Work silently alone
- Collect and grade later

---

## Slide 9: Artifact 5 - The Quiz
- Delivered through Google Classroom
- Questions are randomized
- Immediate feedback to instructor
- Not a gotcha - a signal for what to reteach

---

## Slide 10: The System
Slides support the reading.
Reading prepares discussion.
Discussion deepens understanding.
Quiz measures if it worked.

Remove one piece, the whole system weakens.

---
