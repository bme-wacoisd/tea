# Lesson Plan: The Professional Boundary

**Course**: Grow Your Own Educator Preparation Program
**Grade Level**: 9-12 (Future Educators)
**Duration**: 35-40 minutes
**Instructor**: Brian Edwards (brian.edwards@wacoisd.org)
**Date Prepared**: December 17, 2024

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
1. Identify the three standards of the Texas Educators' Code of Ethics
2. Distinguish between clear ethical violations and situations requiring judgment
3. Apply the "newspaper test" to evaluate potential boundary situations
4. Explain why professional boundaries protect both students and teachers

## Lesson Sequence

### Opening (5 minutes)
- Present scenario: "A teacher and student are texting regularly about the student's personal problems. The teacher thinks they're helping. Is this okay?"
- Take quick poll: appropriate or not?
- Introduce the complexity: most ethics issues aren't black and white

### Direct Instruction (10 minutes)
- Present the three standards of the Code of Ethics
- Distinguish clear violations from gray areas
- Walk through two dilemma scenarios (The Secret, The Favor)
- Explain the "slippery slope" pattern
- Introduce the newspaper test
- Discuss the cost of colleague silence

### Read-Aloud (5 minutes)
- Read the reading.md passage aloud while students follow along
- Pause at "well-intentioned over-involvement" to discuss

### Group Discussion (12-15 minutes)
- Divide into small groups (3-4 students)
- Distribute worksheet with discussion prompts
- Each group discusses the social media dilemma in depth
- Instructor circulates to hear different perspectives

### Whole Class Debrief (5 minutes)
- Groups share their thinking on the social media question
- Discuss why these boundaries exist (protection, not restriction)
- Address the "caring teacher" tension

### Closing (3 minutes)
- Key message: Ethics protect students AND protect you
- Challenge: Find and read the full Texas Educators' Code of Ethics

---

## Assessment Strategy

- **Formative**: Quality of reasoning during ethical dilemma discussions
- **Summative**: Quiz on Code of Ethics standards and ethical decision-making principles

---

## Differentiation Notes

- **For struggling students**: Provide a simplified summary of the three standards
- **For advanced students**: Research a real case where a teacher lost certification
- **For ELL students**: Pre-teach vocabulary (boundary, confidentiality, dilemma, violation)

---

## Instructor Notes

This topic can feel heavy or scary. Frame it positively: understanding ethics protects future teachers from making mistakes, not just from punishment. The goal is confident, ethical practice.

Use realistic scenarios with genuine tension, not extreme cases. Most students won't encounter dramatic violations, but many will face the gray area situations.
