# Lesson Plan: Technology as a Tool, Not a Toy

**Course**: Grow Your Own Educator Preparation Program
**Grade Level**: 9-12 (Future Educators)
**Duration**: 35-40 minutes
**Instructor**: Brian Edwards (brian.edwards@wacoisd.org)
**Date Prepared**: December 17, 2024

---

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
1. Identify and describe the four levels of the SAMR model
2. Distinguish between Enhancement (Substitution/Augmentation) and Transformation (Modification/Redefinition)
3. Evaluate technology use by asking "What can students do now that they couldn't before?"
4. Apply the SAMR framework to AI tools in education

## Lesson Sequence

### Opening (5 minutes)
- Present scenario: "A teacher replaces paper worksheets with an iPad app. Is this 'technology integration'?"
- Collect initial responses
- Introduce the SAMR model as a framework for evaluation

### Direct Instruction (10 minutes)
- Explain the four SAMR levels with clear examples
- Clarify Enhancement (S, A) vs. Transformation (M, R)
- Show how the same tool can operate at different levels depending on use
- Apply SAMR to AI tools (ChatGPT at each level)
- Emphasize: start with learning objectives, not technology

### Read-Aloud (5 minutes)
- Read the reading.md passage aloud while students follow along
- Pause at the AI examples for discussion

### Group Discussion (12-15 minutes)
- Divide into small groups (3-4 students)
- Distribute worksheet with discussion prompts
- Key discussion: Designing AI use at Substitution vs. Redefinition levels
- Instructor circulates to ensure accurate understanding

### Whole Class Debrief (5 minutes)
- Share examples from "Moving Up the Ladder" discussion
- Discuss equity implications of technology requirements
- Address the "because we should" pressure

### Closing (3 minutes)
- Key message: The goal is learning, not being high-tech
- SAMR is a toolbox, not a ladder to climb
- The essential question: "What can students do now that they couldn't before?"

---

## Assessment Strategy

- **Formative**: Accuracy of SAMR level identification during discussions
- **Summative**: Quiz on SAMR levels, examples, and the learning-first principle

---

## Differentiation Notes

- **For struggling students**: Provide SAMR level quick-reference card with examples
- **For advanced students**: Research TPACK framework as a complementary model
- **For ELL students**: Pre-teach vocabulary (substitution, augmentation, modification, redefinition, transformation)
