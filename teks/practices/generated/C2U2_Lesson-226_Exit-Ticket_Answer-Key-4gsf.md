- Which of the following are components of a classroom behavior system?

- Acknowledge and reward positive behaviors immediately
- Define purpose
- Define behavioral expectations, procedures, and consequences
- All of the above

- Which of the following is NOT a potential drawback to classroom reward systems?

- Often focus on extrinsic motivation
- Potentially time-consuming for teachers
- Can distract from learning
- Provides a way for parents to track student behavior

- What are potential benefits to using a classroom reward system?

Positive behavior is reinforced; promotes positive and appropriate behavior; increased motivation; can provide a way for parents to track student behavior


- Intrinsic motivation or extrinsic motivation: Which is more likely to promote long-term impact and why?

- Intrinsic motivation is more likely to promote long-term impacts because students take ownership of their learning and can make real world connections to achieving a reachable goal. Extrinsic motivation can cause students to become dependent on the reward, whereas intrinsic motivation creates satisfaction in completion of the task.



- How can a teacher use Radar; clear, specific, observable directions; and nonverbal interventions to help foster a successful classroom behavior system?

By providing clear, specific, and observable directions students will know exactly what is expected of them and are more likely to meet those expectations; a teacher can scan and praise students who are exhibiting targeted behaviors; a teacher can redirect students who are not on task by using subtle cues or gestures.
