## Slide 4

### Vocabulary


| Guided Practice | A form of practice in which students practice the new skill or concept with support from the teacher and/or peers. |
| --- | --- |
| Independent Practice | A form of practice in which students independently apply what they have learned without support from the teacher or peers. |
| Deliberate Practice | Practice that performs the learning goal (authentic), is spaced over time, is low-stakes and is paired with feedback for improvement |
| Authentic | The practice requires the student to perform the actual learning goal. |
| Low stakes | An activity has little or no impact on the student’s grade. |
| Feedback | Practice of giving students information about their performance against a standard with the purpose of improving student performance |
| Debrief | The teacher facilitates a conversation after an activity has taken place that outlines key questions to drive the group’s learning. |

## Slide 7

### Deliberate Practice: Practice can be guided (with teacher support) or independent (without teacher support)

- Examples
- Skip count as a class
- Use counters to make equal groups. Add the groups and write a number sentence.
- Write repeated addition sentences to represent situations (4+4+4=12)
- Explain to a partner about how 3x4 =12 is related to 4+4+4 =12
- Write multiplication sentences from equal groups
- Complete a set of practice problems with a partner
- Complete a set of practice problems independently
- Non-Examples
- Create and color a foldable to remember the lesson’s vocabulary. Cut and glue it into a math notebook
- Write a poem or rap about multiplication
- Talk to a partner about where you see multiplication in real life
- Watch a video to learn a multiplication song and dance
