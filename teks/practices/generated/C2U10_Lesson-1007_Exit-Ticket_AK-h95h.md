- Why is collaboration so important as an educator?  List at least two benefits of collegial collaboration.
Collaboration is an expectation of professional behavior, and it contributes significantly to the growth of a teacher.
Benefits of collegial collaboration include:
- Helps to build relationships
- Creates a sense of shared responsibility
- Strengthens each other
- Increases knowledge
- Increases student achievement

- Identify and explain at least three common practices for collegial collaboration.



| Professional Learning Communities (PLCs) | Practice of pairing teachers together in a classroom to share the responsibilities of planning, instructing, and assessing students. |
| --- | --- |
| Grade level teams | Voluntary group committed to improving their practice through collaborative learning by utilizing coaches and specific protocols to guide sessions. |
| Peer Observations | Disciplinary teams that collectively analyze student data in relation to specific learning targets and utilize that data to inform and assess effectiveness of instruction. |
| Critical Friends Groups | A teacher observes another teachers classroom for the purpose of learning from their instructional practices. |
| Co-Teaching | Interdisciplinary teams that work together at a specified time to discuss students, plan activities, and coordinate common practices and procedures |
