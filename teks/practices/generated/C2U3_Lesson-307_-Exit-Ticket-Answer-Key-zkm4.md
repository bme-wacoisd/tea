## Exit Ticket - Answer Key

### Lesson 3.07: Maintaining an Asset-Based Mindset through Student Funds of Knowledge

Name:
Date:
Describe and give examples of student funds of knowledge.
The accumulated academic and personal knowledge that students bring to school that are a result of their social and cultural background and their life experiences.  A student’s home language, their cultural traditions, their life experience (like cooking or family activities) might all be funds of knowledge.
What three ideas big ideas did we learn about that are important for developing instruction that reflects students’ funds of knowledge?
Know that funds of knowledge exist for all students: Students’ experiences outside of school are valuable sources of knowledge for the whole class. Every student has them.
Be attentive to students: Pay attention and look for knowledge and skills that students may possess. Listen to them and watch for non-verbal reactions when things interest or engage them.
Show sincere interest in students’ knowledge and experience: Demonstrate that you are excited to learn more about their life and experiences.

Describe at least two ways that teachers can call on students’ funds of knowledge.
Examples may include:
- Encouraging students to share about themselves regularly
Asking for multiple perspectives on an idea
Asking for multiple ways to solve problems
Assigning a student expert on a topic
Using classroom jobs to allow students to use their strengths
Selecting examples and nonexamples from students’ interests and hobbies
Allowing student choice
