## Slide 4

### Vocabulary


| Formal Feedback | Feedback that is planned and systematically scheduled into the learning process.  It is provided consistently for each student. |
| --- | --- |
| Written Feedback | Formal feedback that is provided to as student in written form after a task has been completed. |

- 4

---

## Slide 5

### Characteristics of Effective Feedback

- 5
- Specific and clear
- Focused on the task, not the student
- Identifies both strengths and areas of improvement
- Timely and actionable

---

## Slide 6

### Model: Effective Formal, Written Feedback


|  | Examples | Non-Examples |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Strengths | You included 5 complete sentences You included at least three details Your title is interesting and descriptive Your drawing helps to visualize the event | You wrote sentences You included some details Your title is good. You are a good drawer. |
| Areas of Improvement | You could improve by using periods to end the sentence or thought You could improve by capitalizing only proper nouns and the beginning of sentences Be sure that all your sentences are complete with a subject and verb. | You didn’t use appropriate punctuation. Use complete sentences. |
