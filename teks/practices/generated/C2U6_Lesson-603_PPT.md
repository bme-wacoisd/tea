## Slide 3

### Bellringer

- Look at the objective and the two scenarios and answer the questions in your Student Guide.
- Objective: Students will examine primary and secondary sources to analyze various perspectives on the events that led to the surrender at Appomattox Court House
- Scenario 1: Students perform a historical reenactment of the surrender at the Appomattox Court House. Students research historically accurate costumes and create props for the characters. On the day of the reenactment, students play the role of General Grant and General Lee with a great deal of enthusiasm and emotion. After the reenactment, students engaged in a writing assignment to reflect on how well they portrayed the characters.
- Scenario 2: Students prepare to perform a historical reenactment of the surrender at the Appomattox Court House. Students analyze primary and secondary sources of the event. After the reenactment, students give each other feedback on the accuracy of their portrayal of General Grant and General Lee based on their research. Lastly, students engage in a writing assignment to compare two different perspectives from the event.

## Slide 5

### Standards & Objectives

- Standards: The learning goals or expectations for what students should know and be able to do at each grade level.
- Objectives: Brief statements that describe what students will be expected to learn by the end of the unit or lesson.
