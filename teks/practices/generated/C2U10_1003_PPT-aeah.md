## Slide 5

### ESF Framework: Levers of Effective Schools

- 5

| Lever |  | Description |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1- | Strong School Leadership and Planning | Effective school instructional leaders with clear roles and responsibilities develop and implement improvement plans that address the causes of low performance. |
| 2- | Strategic Staffing | Campus leadership ensured that all students have access to high-quality educators by retaining effective, well-supported teachers through strategic hiring, placement, training, and onboarding |
| 3- | Positive School Culture | Positive school culture requires aligned vision, mission, goals and values, explicit behavioral expectations and management systems, proactive and responsive student support services, and involved families and community. |
| 4- | High-Quality Instructional Materials and Assessments | All students engage daily with TEKS-aligned, high-quality instructional materials, and assessments that support learning at appropriate levels of rigor. |
| 5- | Effective Instruction | Campus leaders provide teachers with job-embedded professional development and access to time and data needed to reflect, adjust, and deliver instruction that meets the needs of all students. |

## Slide 6

### Practice: ESF Essential Actions Stations

- Use the stations to complete each table in your student guide. Each station includes the essential actions taken by schools to ensure they are achieving each lever, and a scenario to determine the effectiveness of an example school, based on the framework.
- Each scenario has the same two questions:
- What are the strengths of this school, based on this lever of the Effective Schools Framework?
- What might the school leader want to improve to ensure that their school is highly effective?
- You will have 5 minutes at each station.
