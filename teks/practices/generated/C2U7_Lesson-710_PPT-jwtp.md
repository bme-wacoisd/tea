## Slide 4

### Vocabulary


| Student Generated Questions | Questions developed by students to help demonstrate their understanding of the content being taught and direct their own learning to understanding more about the content. |
| --- | --- |
| Question-Answer-Relationship (QAR) | A strategy to help students understand the relationship between a question and its answer. |
| Right There Questions | Explicit questions whose answers can be found in the text. Often the words used in the question are the same words found in the text. |
| Think and Search Questions | Explicit questions whose answers are gathered from several parts of the text and put together to make meaning. |
| Author and Me | The answer to the question cannot be found directly in the text. The learner must combine information provided in the text with their own prior knowledge to create a response. |
| On My Own | The answer to the question cannot be found directly in the text. These questions require the learner to use their background or prior knowledge to answer the question. |

- 4

## Slide 5

### A teacher can help students develop questions by:

- Modeling explicitly how to ask good questions
- Providing structured ways for students to ask questions
- Setting expectations that asking questions is the norm in the classroom.

## Slide 6

### Right There Questions


| Definition | Explicit questions whose answers can be found in the text. Often the words used in the question are the same words found in the text. |
| --- | --- |
| Question Starts With | Who What When Where How |
| Example | Where did the story take place? |
| High or Low Level | Low Level |
| Open or Closed | Closed |

## Slide 7

### Think and Search


| Definition | Explicit questions whose answers are gathered from several parts of the text and put together to make meaning. |
| --- | --- |
| Question Starts With | How did…? Summarize Describe Compare What caused…? |
| Example | Why was Baby Bear upset? |
| High or Low Level | Low Level |
| Open or Closed | Closed |

- 7

---

## Slide 8

### Author & Me


| Definition | The answer to the question cannot be found directly in the text. The learner must combine information provided in the text with their own prior knowledge to create a response. |
| --- | --- |
| Question Starts With | Predict what will happen Why did the author…? What is the main idea? |
| Example | What kind of person is Goldilocks? |
| High or Low Level | High Level |
| Open or Closed | Open |

- 8

## Slide 9

### On My Own


| Definition | The answer to the question cannot be found directly in the text. These questions require the learner to use their background or prior knowledge to answer the question. |
| --- | --- |
| Question Starts With | What is your opinion about…? How would you feel…? What would you do…? |
| Example | Goldilocks sat in Baby Bear’s chair and broke it. Write about a time you broke or damaged something that did not belong to you. |
| High or Low Level | High Level |
| Open or Closed | Open |
