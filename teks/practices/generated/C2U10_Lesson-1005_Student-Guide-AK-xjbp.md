| What are the different ways of becoming a teacher in Texas? | Degree Program and Texas Certification  “Traditional Pathway” Individuals complete a traditional Educator Preparation Program in undergraduate and complete their certification requirements simultaneously. 4-year program.  Texas Alternative Certification Process “Alternative Certification Pathway” Individuals complete the requirements of becoming a teacher while they are teaching through an approved Alternative Certification Process entity. This is traditionally a one-year program.  Residency  A paid, intensive year-long clinical experience that pairs an aspiring teacher with a qualified mentor offered by institutes of higher education and alternative certification programs. In a residency program: The resident (student teacher) coteaches with a qualified mentor. The resident receives support from their EPP and from the district during the residency. A sample resident schedule is to coteach 3 days a week and sub or work as a para the other two days. In this way, the resident earns a salary. Residencies are not offered by every program in the state but are growing. |


| Educator Preparation Program (EPP) | A teacher preparation program through an institution or organization that provides aspiring educators with the initial training and requirements to become a teacher. |

| Bachelor’s Degree | A four-year degree received from an accredited undergraduate institution. |

| Internship | A paid and supervised classroom teaching assignment for one year. |


| 3 Main Requirements to Becoming a Teacher | Rationale |

| 1. Have a bachelor’s degree | Individuals receive a well-rounded education in various subjects, and in specific subject. | 2. Clinical teaching or internship | Individuals practice teaching with a certified master teacher in a classroom setting to hone and refine the skills, activities, and procedures necessary for being a successful teacher. |
