## Slide 6

### Vocabulary

- Overload: The situation in which the demands placed on a person by mental work (cognitive load) are greater than the person's mental abilities can cope with
- Sequencing: The effective ordering of academic content to support students' understanding of a topic; sequencing also relies on building on prior knowledge to ensure that there are not gaps in student learning
- Scaffolding: Structured supports offered to students by the teacher to help students attain a strong command of a new skill and/or concept

## Slide 7

### Vocabulary

- Cognitive Load: The relative demand imposed by a particular task in terms of mental resources required (also called “mental load” or “mental workload”)
- Worked Examples: Step-by-step demonstrations of how to perform a task or solve a problem
