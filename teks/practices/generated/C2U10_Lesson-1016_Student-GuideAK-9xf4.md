In this lesson you will receive peer feedback on your personal philosophy statement.  Review your personal philosophy statement and identify a specific question or area on which you would like to receive feedback from a peer.
Responses will vary.

What is the purpose of receiving peer feedback on your personal philosophy statement?
To provide a way for you to receive feedback on areas of strength as well as areas to refine and improve it before it is finalized.
What is the T-A-G strategy?  What does it stand for?
T-A-G is a strategy for providing peer feedback.
T = Tell me something you liked
A =Ask a question
G = Give a suggestion

Success Criteria for Personal Philosophy of Education Statement



| Components | Success Criteria |
| --- | --- |
| Introduction | Clearly articulates a “why” for pursuing a teaching career and why being a teacher matters.  Provides at least 2 supporting details for the why. |
| Beliefs & Values | Clearly articulates at least 3 things that describe effective teaching practices or what good teachers do |
| Strengths | Provides 2-3 areas of personal strength that will support effective teaching.  Describes how these are demonstrated through experience. |
| Areas of Growth | Provides 2-3 areas in which additional growth or learning may be needed.  Describes how these areas of growth were identified. |
| Closing | Shares future goals or aspirations as an educator, including grade level/content that you would like to teach |
