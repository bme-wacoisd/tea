### Vocabulary


| Redirection | Proactive teaching strategy used to engage a student in an alternative behavior. |
| --- | --- |
| Reframing | Saying or doing something again, but in a different way. |
| Emotional Constancy | Manage your tone and body language to keep something positive |
| Economy of Language | Using few words to remain focused on the task at hand |
| Positive Framing | Putting a positive perspective on any event, especially one that appears on the surface and at the present to be negative |

## Slide 8

### Assume the Best

- Attributes behavior to positive intent: When students are making a good-faith effort, avoid ascribing ill intention to what could be the result of distraction or genuine misunderstanding
- Is specific about what you want students to do: Hold students accountable for following through by ensuring they know exactly what they should be doing
- Maintains Economy of Language: Use few words
- Maintains Emotional Constancy: It’s best to use a neutral tone and facial expression when delivering redirections of any kind

## Slide 9

### Go-To Phrases


| Type | Example |
| --- | --- |
| Student Excitement | “Some of us are so excited that we’re already jumping in. Before you start, please make sure your desks are totally clear.” |
| Student Forgetting | “Pause. A couple of us forgot to complete planning before drafting. Please compete parts 1 and 2 of the prep sheet first.” |
| Teacher Clarity | “I wasn’t clear enough. First, be sure to jot down your own word or phrase as part of your annotation.” |
| Student Confusion | “It seems like a few of us might be confused. Let me model one more before you go back to independent work.” |
