### Vocabulary

- 4

| Term | Definition |
| --- | --- |
| Degree Program and Texas Certification or “Traditional Pathway” | Individuals complete a traditional Educator Preparation Program in undergraduate and complete their certification requirements simultaneously. 4-year program. |
| Educator Preparation Program (EPP) | A teacher preparation program through an institution or organization that provides aspiring educators with the initial training and requirements to become a teacher. |
| Alternative Certification Process or “Alternative Certification Pathway” | Individuals complete the requirements of becoming a teacher while they are teaching through an approved Alternative Certification Process entity. This is traditionally a one-year program. |
| Residency | A paid, intensive year-long clinical experience that pairs an aspiring teacher with a qualified mentor. |
| Bachelor’s Degree | A four-year degree received from an accredited undergraduate institution. |
| Certification Exam | A test used to determine whether individuals are knowledgeable enough in a specific subject to work in that subject area. |
| Clinical Teaching | Student teaching experience where students are supervised by a certified teacher in a public-school classroom. |
| Internship | A paid and supervised one-year classroom teaching assignment. |

### Notes: Becoming a Classroom Teacher in Texas

- Degree Program and Texas Certification or “Traditional Pathway”

## Slide 6

### Notes: Becoming a Classroom Teacher in Texas

- Residency Pathway
- Offered by both institutes of higher education and alternative certification pathways is a residency program.
- A residency is a paid, intensive year-long clinical experience that pairs an aspiring teacher with a qualified mentor.
- In a residency program:
- The resident (student teacher) coteaches with a qualified mentor.
- The resident receives support from their EPP and from the district during the residency
- A sample resident schedule is to coteach 3 days a week and sub or work as a para the other two days. In this way, the resident earns a salary.
- Residencies are not offered by every program in the state but are growing.

## Slide 8

### Notes: Becoming a Classroom Teacher in Texas

- Reasons theses are requirements to becoming a teacher:
- A bachelor’s degree is a requirement because it helps:
- Individuals receive a well-rounded education in various subjects, and in specific subject.
- Individuals strengthen critical thinking skills, creativity, work ethic, problem-solving ability, written and verbal communication techniques, among others.
- This prepares teachers to have a strong knowledge for a specific subject or grade-level, and to have the skillsets to interact and guide students successfully.
- Clinical teaching or internships are a requirement because they help:
- Individuals practice and receive feedback on teaching from a mentor teacher.
- Passing a certification exam is a requirement because:
- To test if the teacher has adequate knowledge in their subject area and ensure they are qualified to teach that subject.
