## Slide 4

### Vocabulary

- Rapport: A relationship characterized by mutual understanding or empathy that makes communication possible or easy.
- Authentic Listening: A strategy to build rapport with students that includes the teacher giving full attention to conversations, demonstrating empathy, and suspending judgment so that the students can be fully heard.

## Slide 6

### Authentic Listening Components

- Give Full Attention
- Limiting distractions, like putting away cell phones or closing laptops, show the speaker that you are fully engaged and present in the conversation.
- Asking genuine, direct follow-up questions also shows the speaker that you care and are invested in the conversation.
- Demonstrate Empathy
- Demonstrating empathy means that you try to understand feelings as well as words.
- Being kind, compassionate, and empathetic can allow students to feel more at ease.
- 6

---

## Slide 7

### Authentic Listening Components

- Suspend Judgment
- Suspending judgment is important so that students do not feel like their words or emotions are being judged or thought of as “less than.”
- Listen with an open mind to students’ concerns.
- Accept Natural Student Expression
- Let students express themselves in their own way.
- There is no need to correct grammar when listening to students to build trust and rapport.
- Allowing students to come to you as their authentic selves can improve rapport and build deeper trust.

## Slide 8

### Authentic Listening Components

- Empathy Sentence Starters
- It sounds to me like this might feel….
- I can sense that you are feeling…
- I can hear how [emotion] you are feeling.
- Your face is telling me that…
- I can hear in your voice that…
- Affirming Statements
- Thank you for sharing this with me.
- I understand you feel that way.
- That sounds like a(n) [adjective] experience.
- I hear you.
- I am not sure what to say right now, but I am here to listen.
