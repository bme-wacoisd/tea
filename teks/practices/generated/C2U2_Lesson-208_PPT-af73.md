## Slide 7

### Challenge Success Criteria

- Inspires students: Exhort students to prove what they can do by building competition into the day.
- Maintains Economy of Language: Using fewer words helps students to focus on what they need to do to meet expectations and brings everyone back to the lesson as quickly as possible.
- Maintains Emotional Constancy: Again, it’s wise to use a neutral tone when delivering redirections of any kind.

### Ways to Challenge Students


| Challenge Type | Description | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Self | Communicate that you believe in a student by challenging them to compete against themselves |  |
| Others | Inspire friendly competition by challenging your students to compete against each other |  |
| Goals & Standards | Reference an external goal or standard to which students might strive |  |
| The Clock | Encourage students to use every moment effectively |  |

## Slide 9

### Ways to Challenge Students-Sentence Starters


| Challenge Type | Description | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Self | Communicate that you believe in a student by challenging them to compete against themselves | “This sentence is good, but I think you can make it even better.” |
| Others | Inspire friendly competition by challenging your students to compete against each other | “The Blue Group is killing it. Hope everyone else brought their A game!” |
| Goals & Standards | Reference an external goal or standard to which students might strive | “When writing, we always strive for precision. Go back and see if you can be even more precise.” |
| The Clock | Encourage students to use every moment effectively | “Three minutes left. See how many verbs you can improve before the bell.” |
