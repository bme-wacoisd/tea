- Define formative assessment and summative assessment and explain the purpose of each.
Formative: Low or no-stakes assessments that gather data about a small number (1-2) of learning targets. Formative assessment data is collected throughout a learning sequence (beginning, middle, and end) to inform a teacher’s next steps.
Summative: High-stakes or graded assessments, given at the end of a learning sequence, that gather data about student mastery of multiple learning targets.
