Practice 2: Definitions and Strategies










| Classroom Management | Classroom Management | The skills and techniques that teachers use to create a safe, organized, and positive learning environment. | The skills and techniques that teachers use to create a safe, organized, and positive learning environment. |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
|  |  | Definition | Strategies |
| Positive Teacher & Student Relationships | High Expectations | Actions based on the belief that all students can achieve at high levels for academics and behavior. | Call on all students, ask higher-order questions, demand effort and engagement, encourage productive struggle |
| Positive Teacher & Student Relationships | Calm & Consistent                    Teacher Actions | Actions that ensure a safe learning environment and are implemented with regularity and fairness for all students. | Explicit and enforced rules and procedures, calm tone, procedures and routines |
| Proactive | Behavior Management | The processes or procedures that encourage students to engage in positive behavior that is essential for successful teaching and learning. | Radar, Be Seen, Do Not Talk Over |
| Proactive | Rules, Systems, & Procedures | The structures that set expectations for how a class operates and interacts. | How to enter and exit the classroom, how to access materials, Bellringer routines, reinforced and consistent classroom rules |
| Proactive | Instructional Design | The intentional development of engaging learning tasks that are relevant, interesting, and appropriately challenging to students | Connecting lessons to student interest, engaging and high-interest materials |
| Proactive | Classroom Design | The purposeful arrangement and physical layout of a classroom to achieve desired teaching and learning outcomes. | Space between desks for safe movement, teacher access to all students |
| Responsive | Positive Reinforcement | A response following a desired behavior that is intended to make the behavior more likely to occur in the future. | Noticing appropriate behavior |
| Responsive | Proximity Control | A strategy in which a teacher moves towards and stands near a student who may be off task as a reminder of expected behavior. | Moving closer to students who are talking, moving closer to students who are off task |
| Responsive | Redirection | The act of helping a student to focus on whatever your class is being asked to do at a given point in time. | Private correction, least intrusive strategies |
| Responsive | Intervention | A specific program or set of steps to address an academic or behavioral need. | Private correction, contracts |

Teacher then asks additional students to evaluate and/or add to the initial answer.
