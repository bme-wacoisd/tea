How do least invasive interventions help create a strong classroom culture?
Using least invasive interventions creates a safe classroom space where students feel they can take risks. Students are held accountable for upholding high expectations which communicates to them that you believe in their ability to achieve success. Least invasive interventions make student redirection and intervention nearly invisible so that teaching and learning is not disrupted.   A teacher can also easily maintain anonymity when using least invasive interventions, which promotes a safe environment.
