## Slide 4

### Curriculum & HQIM

- 4
- Curriculum: A sequenced collection of instructional materials such as lesson plans, learning tasks, and assessments.
- Curriculum is used by teachers to guide students towards proficiency in content knowledge and skills.
- High Quality Instructional Materials (HQIM): Instructional materials that are research based, closely aligned to rigorous standards, and reviewed by a team of experts.

## Slide 10

### How do teachers know what to teach or what should be in a curriculum?

- Standards: The expectations for what students should know and be able to do by the end of each grade level.
- Texas Essential Knowledge and Skills (TEKS): The Texas state learning standards. The TEKS outline what students in Texas should know and be able to do at each grade level.
- English Language Proficiency Standards (ELPS): These standards outline the instruction that must be provided to allow English language learners the full opportunity to learn English and succeed academically.

## Slide 14

### Components of a Curriculum

- Units:
- Groups of learning standards are taught in units or modules
- A unit is a collection of daily lessons and assessments around a broad topic
- Each unit has goals or essential questions and a summative assessment
- Lesson Plans:
- Units are divided into daily lesson plans
- Daily lesson plans target specific objects that support achievement of unit goals and standards
- Lesson plans include targeted objectives, learning activities, and formative assessments
