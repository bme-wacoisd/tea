## Slide 4

### Vocabulary


| Cover Letter | A letter which accompanies an application or resume in which an applicant explains why they are interested in the opportunity for which they’re applying. |
| --- | --- |
| Purpose Statement | A short essay, like an admission essay, which concentrates on future goals as opposed to past experiences. |

## Slide 5

### Key Consideration #1

- Research the Position/Program/Place/Opportunity
- What do they value?
- What are they looking for?
- How does this align with your values and what you want to do?
- 5

---

## Slide 6

### Key Consideration #2

- 2. Examine Your Skills
- List the skills and experiences that match the values and needs of the program or position.
- Determine how your skills and experience can make that program/position better.
- 6

---

## Slide 7

### Key Consideration #3

- 3. Top Three Skills
- Review the list of skills you created in step 2 and select three that you feel most strongly align with the values and needs of the organization as well as with your goals.
- These three skills will inform your body paragraphs in your personal statement.
- 7

---

## Slide 8

### Exit Ticket

- Return to your personal statement that received peer feedback.
- What changes are you going to make to your personal statement? How will these changes emphasize your top three skills? Do you feel like there is a skill you have missed out on sharing?
