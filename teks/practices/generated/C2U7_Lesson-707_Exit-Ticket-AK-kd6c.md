Describe three things that teachers can do to structure effective collaborative learning.
Possible responses include:
Teachers can structure collaborative learning by ensuring that the task is designed in a way that collaboration is necessary to accomplish the task.

Teachers can structure collaborative learning by clearly communicating expectations for the collaborative task.

Teachers can structure collaborative learning by utilizing structures, such as group roles and norms, to make sure that students share the responsibility and ownership of outcomes.

Teachers can structure collaborative learning by varying the size and membership of the group according to the activity’s purpose and individual learning goals. Groups are most effective when they range in size from 3-to-5 participants.
