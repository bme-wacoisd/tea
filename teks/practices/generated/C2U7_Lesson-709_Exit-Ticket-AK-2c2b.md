### Lesson 7.09: Questioning | Using Questioning Strategies to Support Student Learning

Draw a line to match each question type with the corresponding definition.

High-Level Question			There are a limited number of acceptable answers.
Low-Level Question			Requires students to analyze, evaluate or create.
Open Question				Requires students to remember, understand, and apply.
Closed Question			There are many acceptable answers.

Analyze the following questions about a story to determine if they are low/high level and open/closed and explain your reasoning.


| Questions | High/Low | Open/Closed |
| --- | --- | --- |
| What happened right after David went into Adam’s room? | Low  Reasoning: students recall information from the story. | Closed  Reasoning: There is only one possible response. |
| Retell the story from a different character’s point of view. | High  Reasoning: Students must think the situation from someone else’s point of view to retell the story | Open  Reasoning: How the student interprets the other person’s point of view may be different. |
| What details tells you what was making the strange sounds in Adam’s closet? | High  Reasoning: Students must analyze the different details in the text to determine which helps them know what is making the sound. This requires students to use prior knowledge. | Closed  Reasoning: There are a limited number of possible details they can use. |
| How would you summarize this story? | Low  Reasoning: This question requires students recall events in the story. | Open  Reasoning: Students can summarize the story in different ways. |
