|  | Note: This lesson may take longer than a class period to complete based on the amount of time students need to complete the research components of the lesson. |  |
| 3 mins | Bellringer: How much do you think it costs to complete a teacher education program in Texas? What opportunities are there to fund your educational pathway? | Student Guide |
| 2 mins | Debrief T: Turn and talk to a partner and discuss your responses to the bellringer.  S: (Turn and talk to partner to discuss responses.)   T: How much do you think it costs to become a teacher in Texas?  S: Responses will vary.  T: What opportunities are there to fund your educational pathway?   S: Responses will vary based on background knowledge but may include scholarships, grants, loans, etc. | Student Guide |
| 15 mins | Costs Associated with Becoming a Teacher  T: Today, we are going to explore the typical costs associated with becoming a Texas teacher and various ways to fund your educational pathway.   T: First, let’s look at the costs associated with becoming a teacher. What are some of the things you think you will have to pay for to become a teacher?  S: College, certification exams, applications, books  T: Do all colleges cost the same to attend?  S: No  T: I want you to think about a particular educator prep program or college program you are interested in attending. You are going to research the typical costs associated with completing that program and record them in the chart in your Student Guide. Look for things such as the cost of tuition for the duration of the program, etc. without financial aid.   T: Are there any questions?  S: (Ask questions as needed.)  T: You will have 10 minutes to explore the costs and record your notes. You may begin.  S: (Complete research and record notes in Student Guide.)  T: (After 10 minutes) Let’s come back together. Who would like to share the cost they found for their selected program?  S: (Share out.)  T: Now, you are going to explore the costs for the certification exams and applications necessary to become an educator in Texas. You may use the resources linked in your Student Guide to help you explore these costs. You will have about 8 minutes.  S: (Complete research and record notes in Student Guide.)  T: (After 8 minutes.) Let’s come back together. Who would like to share the information they found? S: (Share out.) | Student Guide PPT |
| 5 mins | Notes: Scholarships, Student Loans, and Student Loan Forgiveness in Texas T: As you can see, there are some initial costs associated with becoming a teacher. The good news is that there are many different types of opportunities to fund your educational pathway. In just a moment, you are going to explore financial aid opportunities associated with the EPP or college program you research earlier. First, let’s review four different types of financial aid. Please refer to your vocabulary section in your Student Guide.  S: (Find vocabulary section in Student Guide.)  T: (Share vocabulary via PPT.)  T:  Scholarship: Money that does not need to be repaid based on academic merit, talent, or area of study. Student Loan: Money borrowed from a bank to attend a college or career school that must be repaid, usually with interest. Student Loan Forgiveness: A program where the government may forgive part or all your federal student loans.  Grant: A form of financial aid that doesn’t have to be repaid unless you do not fulfill the necessary requirements.  S: (Write vocabulary in Student Guide.) | Student Guide PPT |
| 13 mins | Practice: Financial Aid Opportunities T: You are now going to research different financial aid opportunities for the specific program you explored earlier.   T: Find the section titled “Practice: Financial Aid Opportunities” in your student guide.  S: (Locate section in Student Guide.)  T: In the research table, you will explore the EPP website and identify financial aid opportunities offered by the program. You will name the financial aid opportunity and provide a brief description. You can use secondary websites and resources to identify additional financial aid opportunities.   T: Let’s look at an example. Please take a minute to observe the example in your Student Guide.   S: (Silently read the example.)   T: What do you notice about the example?  S: Responses may vary.   T: (Record the responses on the board for students to reference during their research table activity.)   T: Are there any clarifying questions?  S: (Ask clarifying questions.)   T: You will have 15 minutes. You may begin. | Student Guide |
| 2 mins | Debrief T: What were some of the financial aid opportunities you explored?  S: Responses will vary.  T: Why is it important for aspiring teachers to be aware of the different financial aid opportunities?  S: Many may think it isn’t possible for them to pursue a career in education because they can’t afford it, but once you are aware of the different financial aid opportunities, you can see that it is possible. |  |
| 5 mins | S: (Complete the Exit Ticket.) | Exit Ticket |
