## Slide 6

### Vocabulary

- Working Memory: Temporary memory that must be constantly activated to maintain.
- Long-Term Memory: Stored information that can be retrieved even after long periods of inactivity.
- Prior Knowledge: Information or context a learner already has about a topic before they learn new information. Prior knowledge can help students:
- Make connections to new information
- Better understand new information
- Store and retrieve new information
- 6

## Slide 7

### Vocabulary

- Misconception: An incorrect understanding that students bring to new learning.
- Prior Knowledge Activities: Activities that draw upon students' prior knowledge, giving teachers a way to identify misconceptions and understand what students already know about a given topic.
- KWL Chart: A strategy for assessing prior knowledge that asks students what they already know and what they want to know. It can be used throughout a unit as students complete the final column about what they have learned.

## Slide 8

### Three Steps for Using Prior Knowledge in Teaching

- Determine what prior knowledge is necessary for students to access.
- Determine if students have the prior knowledge they need for the lesson.
- Plan for how to support students if they do not have that prior knowledge.
