### Lesson 1.06: Planning and Delivering Effective Directions

Think of an effective teacher you have experienced. Think beyond the content of the directions they provided to consider the way they delivered the directions. Describe how the teacher looked and sounded when delivering instructions.
Student examples may vary but may include:
They spoke in a clear voice.
They looked at the students while speaking.
They spoke slowly.



Key Vocabulary
Define each key vocabulary term below.

When a teacher delivers instructions well, they use a slower pace, an even cadence, a lower pitch, a quieter volume, and a serious tone. The teacher should also stand still when delivering instructions.
