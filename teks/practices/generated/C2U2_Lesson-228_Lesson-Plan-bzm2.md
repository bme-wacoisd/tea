| 5 mins | Bellringer: Use your Classroom Management Plan Template and the Classroom Management Plan Rubric in your Student Guide to reflect on your progress with your plan relating to each of the five components: Classroom Rules Classroom Procedures Classroom Layout Behavior Management & Intervention Positive Relationship Development How would you rate your progress in each of these areas? | Student Guide |
| 2 mins | Resuming Classroom Management Plan Instructions T: Today you will return to your individual Classroom Management Plan, using the template from the last lesson to address all components of effective classroom management that include the following sections: Classroom Rules, emphasizing: Simplicity Clarity Positive Framing Student Ownership Classroom Procedures, including: Morning/Beginning of Class Routines Routines for Managing Work Dismissal/End of Class Routines Classroom Layout, accounting for: Smooth Transitions Whole Group Discussion Small Group Work Access to Materials Behavior Management & Intervention, including: Preventative Strategies Reactive Techniques Reward Systems Positive Relationship Development, including: Trust and Rapport Boundaries Demeanor Consistency High Expectations Strong Voice Formal Register T: You will have fifteen minutes to complete your classroom management plan. | Student Guide |
| 15 mins | Developing a Classroom Management Plan S: (Independently work on Classroom Management Plan.) T: (Assist students as needed.) | Student Guide |
| 15 mins | Classroom Management Plan Feedback T: Now that you’ve spent some additional time on your Classroom Management Plans, you will engage with a neighbor to give and receive feedback on your plans. Use the Classroom Management Plan Rubric in your Student Guide to provide feedback on where your partner can “grow” their plan and how their plan may exceed the rubric’s expectation (“glows”).  S: (Work with a near neighbor as a partner to review one another’s Classroom Management Plans, using the Rubric from the Student Guide to guide the conversation. Students should spend approximately seven minutes per Classroom Management Plan to ensure that both partners get feedback.) | Student Guide |
| 3 mins | Debrief T: Who is willing to share some “glows” that you and your partner discussed?  S: Answers will vary.  T: Great! And what about areas of growth?   S: Answers will vary. | Student Guide |
