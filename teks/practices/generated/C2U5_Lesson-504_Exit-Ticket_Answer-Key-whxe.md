| Mr. M. wants to get a sense of what his class understands about a topic before a lesson, but he also wants to hear potential misconceptions. What informal assessment strategy might he use? | Think-Pair-Share or Four Corners | Both activities will provide an opportunity for Mr. M. not only to identify student answers to questions, but also to hear the thinking behind their answers. | In the middle of his lesson, Mr. M. wants to get a sense of how overloaded his students might be and whether they are ready for the next step of the lesson which will introduce new content. What informal assessment strategy might he use? | Thumbs up, down, sideways | Thumbs up, down, sideways will allow Mr. M. to get a sense of how many students are ready to move on. If too many students are feeling partially or completely confused, he might want to slow down his pacing to avoid overload. |
