## Slide 5

### Why is Collaborative Learning a High Impact Teaching Strategy?

- Collaborative learning develops higher-level thinking, verbal communication, self-management, and leadership skills.
- Collaborative learning exposes students to an increase in understanding of different perspectives.

## Slide 6

### Five Key Practices for Structuring Collaborative Learning

- Ensure that the task is designed so that collaboration is necessary to accomplish the task.
- Vary the size and membership of the group according to the activity’s purpose and individual learning goals. Groups are most effective when they range in size from 3-5 participants.
- Use structures such as group roles, norms, and explicit time keeping to ensure that students share the responsibility and ownership of outcomes.
- Provide opportunities for regular feedback on group processes as well as student/group self-evaluation.
- Be clear about expectations and instructions for the task.
