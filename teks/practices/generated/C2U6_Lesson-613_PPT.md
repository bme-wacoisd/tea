### Vocabulary


| Curriculum | A curriculum is a year-long, standards-based guide of instructional practices, learning experiences, and assessments used to facilitate student learning of grade-level concepts. |
| --- | --- |
| Objectives | Brief statements that describe what students will be expected to learn by the end of the unit or lesson |
| Assessment | Any tool used to collect, analyze, and respond to information about student understanding of learning targets |
| Lesson Plan | A teacher’s detailed plan for teaching an individual lesson |
| Norms | Agreements adopted by a group for how it will do something together and/or behave together. |

## Slide 4

### Bellringer: Reviewing Norms

- Norms for Teacher
- Take teaching seriously and stay “in character.”
- Talk to your students in a grade appropriate way.
- Take time to prepare.
- Forgive yourself if at first it seems hard.
- Norms for Role-Playing Students
- Take being a student seriously and stay “in character.”
- Actively respond to the teachers prompts and questions.
- Don’t act out or intentionally misbehave.
- Remember you are a third grader—the content may seem easy to you.
- Extend kindness to your teacher if, at first, they have trouble taking on the teaching role.

## Slide 5

### Rehearsal Plan (17 minutes per role play round)

- 9 minutes: Teach the lesson with the other two classmates acting as the students
- 4 minutes: Record reflections on your experience, using either the teacher reflection table or the student reflection table in your Student Guide
- 2 minutes: Share warm and cool feedback with the teacher
- 2 minutes: Allow the teacher to reflect on teaching the lesson
