## Slide 3

### Bellringer

- Think about a time in your life when you were learning a new skill (such as how to play piano) or were new to a group (such as your first day in a karate class). How did you feel during that time? Did you have anyone who guided you through that process until you felt comfortable on your own?
- If yes, how did that person help? If no, why do you think it could have been helpful to have someone to help guide you?

## Slide 4

### Vocabulary

- Mentor Teacher: An experienced teacher who provides guidance and support to another teacher through observation, collaboration, and a trusting relationship.
- Mentee Teacher: A teacher who receives guidance and support to better their teaching practice.
- 4

---

## Slide 5

### Who are Mentors?

- Usually an experienced teacher (3+ years)
- Demonstrated skills as an accomplished teacher based on evidence of student growth and positive evaluations.
- Good communicators
- Skilled in coaching teachers to better outcomes.
- 5

---

## Slide 6

### What is the Role of a Mentor Teacher?

- Observe and provide feedback to the mentee.
- Model best practices in teaching and learning.
- Help the mentee to establish and meet goals.
- Provide emotional support for the mentee when met with challenging situations at work.
- Encourage habits that promote a healthy work-life balance
- 6

---

## Slide 7

### How do the Mentor Teachers Benefit?

- Develop relationships with their colleagues.
- Continuous focus on best practices in instruction and coaching.
- Fosters a positive school culture
- 7

---

## Slide 8

### Who are Mentees?

- Novice teachers
- Teachers looking for support to better their practice
- 8

---

## Slide 9

### What is the Role of a Mentee Teacher?

- Ask questions and seek guidance.
- Be receptive to feedback.
- Communicate openly about areas of success as well as struggles.
- Work with the mentor to establish and meet goals.
- 9

---

## Slide 10

### How do the Mentee Teachers Benefit?

- Develop relationships with their colleagues.
- Support and guidance with both academic and non-academic concerns
- Faster improvement of instructional practices based on relevant and immediate feedback.
