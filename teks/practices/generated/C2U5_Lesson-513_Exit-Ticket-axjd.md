| Levels | Writing Qualities |
| --- | --- |
| Level 1: Emerging Writer | Begins to use spaces to separate words. Sometimes uses beginning, middle and ending letters. Writing able to be read by child but not by others. Writes familiar words from memory. |
| Level 2: Developing Writer | Uses invented spelling that others can recognize and read. Inconsistent use of capital letters, punctuation and spacing between words. At least one complete thought included. |
| Level 3: Proficient Writer | Spells some words correctly. Begins to use capital letters, punctuation and spacing regularly and correctly. Includes related ideas that focus on one topic, talks more about what happened next or partial story. |
| Level 4: Outstanding Writer | Story is complete and readily understood. Varying sentence patterns and/or descriptive vocabulary Consistently demonstrates appropriate usage of capital letters, punctuation and spacing. Spells grade level appropriate words correctly. |
