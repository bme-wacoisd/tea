Non-Example: Modeling
Students in a 1st grade science class are learning to classify animals by their different characteristics.
Objective:  Students will identify distinguishing characteristics of animal families.
Instructional outcome: I can identify specific characteristics of animal families
Prior learning: Animals can be classified according to:
- physical characteristics such as body covering (fur, scales, hair, feathers, shells, etc.),
- body shape (two main features, three main features)
- appendages (arms, legs, wings, fins, tails) a
- method of movement (walking, crawling, flying, swimming).
- whether they live in water or land (habitat)
- whether they are wild or domestic.
In the model below the teacher is demonstrating to students how to classify g a group of animals based on their characteristics.  After the teacher models the process of classifying, the students will be given pictures of different animals and asked to find examples that fit each of the different categories above.
Script for Modeling the Classification of Animals
T: Today class we are going to work on classifying different animals based on their characteristics.  I am going to demonstrate to you how to think about how we can group these animals based on different characteristics.
T: (Holds up a picture of a tiger.)  This is a picture of an animal called a tiger.  What do you notice about this animal?
S: He has stripes.
T: Yes, he has stripes.  What else do you notice?
S: He has a tail, and he looks mean.
T: Yes, the tiger has a tail.  The tail is a part of physical characteristics that we will examine.  What other physical characteristics do you notice?
S: The tiger is orange and has 4 legs.
T: Yes, good the legs are an important physical characteristic. What do we know about animals that have legs?
S: They walk?
T: Yes, walking is a type of movement that we can use to classify animals.
T: Now, you are going to look for examples of different animals that exhibit different physical characteristics.  I have given each of you several pictures to look at an examine and a chart of the different physical characteristics to look at.  You will work to find examples of each of those characteristics and put the pictures of animals that fit each of the categories under it.  Go ahead and get started now.
