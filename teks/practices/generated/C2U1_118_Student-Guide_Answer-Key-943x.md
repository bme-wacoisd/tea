Why is it important for a teacher to manage their tone and body language when delivering a Do It Again statement?
- You want to reassure the class that mistakes are part of the learning process and that you want to commit to helping students be their best selves.

Why is it important to focus on economy of language when delivering a Do It Again Statement?
- You want to keep students focused on the content.

Why is it important to pause students as close as possible to the moment when you see the class make a mistake?
- People learn best when they get feedback as close to the point of error as possible.

How does the use of these Do It Again techniques build culture and accountability?
They help hold the group accountable in a reasonable and nonpunitive way for the behavior of all students. Students feel they are in a culture where they can hold each other accountable. A classroom culture of compliance and acceptable is replaced with excellence.
How does the use of these Do It Again techniques help ensure students end with success?
They helps build muscle memory by providing students the opportunity to practice expectations repeatedly. Additionally, students end the routine by doing it the right way, which leaves students with the memory of what right looks like.
