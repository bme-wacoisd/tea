Ms. K is a fourth-grade math teacher. She is explaining how to do a problem and is talking loudly over side conversations, the grinding of the pencil sharpener, and the rustling of papers. What is the likely impact on the classroom culture?
Answers will vary but may include the following: Talking over divides student attention and distracts students from the learning making it hard to focus on the most important content. By simply talking over, the teacher communicates to students that what he/she is saying isn’t that important.

What are the four Self-Interrupt Success Criteria?
- Adopt a symmetrical stance and hold still.
- Hold the pause in your speech for one or two seconds to signal that what you’re about to say is of the utmost importance.
- To enhance the strategy, raise your eyebrows.
- Finally, when you do begin speaking again, speak quieter, slower, evenly, and at a slightly lower pitch.

How does Laura Fern effectively use the self-interrupt strategy?

She seamlessly shifts between casual instructional tone, self-interrupts by squaring up and standing still, and resumes teaching as soon as student is paying attention.
