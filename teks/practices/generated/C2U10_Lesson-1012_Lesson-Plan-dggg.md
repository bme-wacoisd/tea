| 5 mins | Bellringer Over the next few class meetings, you will be assembling a portfolio of artifacts to showcase what you have learned as well as the skills you have gained in this course.  Consider what you have learned in this course. What has been most impactful for you? How would you like to showcase that for yourself, your classmates, and others who may see your portfolio? | Student Guide PPT |
| 5 mins | Purpose of the Portfolio T: Who would like to share their reflection from their bellringer today? (Allow a few students to share.) S: (Share out.) T: Today you are going to have the opportunity to start gathering artifacts and assembling your final course portfolio. The purpose of this portfolio is to compile your academic work in a format where you can share it with your classmates, stakeholders, and even carry it through to your practicum course. | Student Guide PPT |
| 5 mins | Portfolio Requirements/Components T: Please open your Student Guide. Here you will find a checklist of the overview of your portfolio as well as the components. Your portfolio will include:  Evidence of skills that you have learned and practiced. Artifacts that you have produced while in Instructional Practices Reflections on what you have learned in this course. Your Personal Philosophy of Education Future Goals T: Let’s look at the components of your portfolio. Each portfolio will include:  Table of Contents Personal Philosophy of Education  What strengths do I bring that will help me become a teacher?  What are my areas of growth? Why does being a teacher matter? What do I believe are the central tenants of good teaching? What do good teachers do? What subjects or grade levels do I want to consider teaching? Video evidence from at least two sample teaching experiences within the class Written reflection on the practice teaching experiences (Number 3) Behavior Management Plan from Unit 2 Written reflections including: Mentee Questions Steps to Becoming a Teacher Chart and Potential Plan Three classroom artifacts (from Instructional Practices) that best represent your work (summative assessments, analysis, classwork, etc.) Your goals for considering and pursuing an education career. T: What questions do you have about the portfolio requirements? S: (Ask clarifying questions.) | Student Guide PPT |
| 25 mins | Portfolio Artifact Selection T: Today we will start by taking an inventory of the artifacts you have available, those that you will need more work on, and those that you have questions about. Throughout the next several classes, we will work together to build your portfolio.  T: For the next 25 minutes, please take some time to look through your checklist of items that are required for your portfolio and begin your inventory. You have a blank inventory sheet in your Student Guide to help you keep track of your progress. I will be walking around and answering questions and helping you during this time. T: Do you have any questions? S: (Ask clarifying questions.) | Student Guide Student Artifacts (will vary) |
