Read the following quote from American philosopher Ralph Waldo Emerson and explain what it means about the teaching profession.
"The great teacher is not the (person) who supplies the most facts, but the one in whose presence we become different people."
Responses may vary but may include:
Teachers can’t just teach facts to their students. They need to help them learn how to think for themselves.
Teachers are role models who help students learn how to be responsible.
Teachers help make their students into better people.


Notes: Teacher Professionalism in Action
Why is it important for teachers to be professional?
Responses may vary but may include:
Because teachers show students how they should behave.
Students spend so much time with their teachers that they see them as role models.
Students learn about how they should act in their jobs from teachers.
What are the characteristics of teachers who act professionally?
