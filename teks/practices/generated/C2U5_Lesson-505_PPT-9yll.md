## Slide 4

### Introduction to Strategic Questioning: Characteristics 1 &2

- Be meaningful and understandable to students (succinct)
- Example: What is one characteristic of this tomoato?
- Non-Example: Given this specimen that you see in front of you, what qualities would you attribute to this delectable fruit?
- Focus on the most important content
- Example: What is one word that describes this tomato?
- Non-Example: Do you like tomatoes? Why or why not?

## Slide 5

### Introduction to Strategic Questioning: Characteristics 3&4

- Align to the level of thinking required in the learning target
- Example: How would you describe the relationship between characteristics and traits?
- Non-Example: What is a characteristic? What is a trait?
- Increase in complexity and demand as learning progresses
- Example: Varying question types throughout the lesson while steadily increasing to get to the highest complexity required by the learning target
- Non-Example: Keeping questioning at the same level of complexity throughout a lesson, starting with the hardest questions at the beginning of a lesson
