## Slide 4

### Vocabulary


| Radar | A classroom management strategy in which the teacher gives clear, observable directions, visually scans the room, and positions themself to be easily seen by students. |
| --- | --- |
| Observable Directions | Directions that allow the teacher to see if students are meeting the expectation |
| Specific | Clearly defined or identified |
| Concrete | Providing specific instructions or actions |
| Scanning | Looking consistently and intentionally across the entire classroom |
| Pastore’s Perch | Observing from the corner of the room where you can see most effectively and proficiently. |

## Slide 7

### Notes: Pastore’s Perch

- Pastor’s Perch helps teacher’s see more of the room
- If a teacher is standing at the center of the room, they will need to scan a field of 150 degrees to see all students
- If a teacher is standing in the corner of the room – Pastore’s Perch – they only need to scan 80 degrees to see all students
- Field of vision is wider from Pastore’s Perch – there are fewer blind spots
