### Lesson 10.12: Professional Portfolio | Introduction & Components

Name:
Date:
Bellringer:
Over the next few class meetings, you will be assembling a portfolio of artifacts to showcase what you have learned as well as the skills you have gained in this course.
Consider what you have learned in this course. What has been most impactful for you? How would you like to showcase that for yourself, your classmates, and others who may see your portfolio?
Answers will vary.

Portfolio Overview:
- Evidence of skills that you have learned and practiced
- Artifacts that you have produced while in Instructional Practices
- Reflections on what you have learned in this course.
- Your Personal Philosophy of Education
- Future Goals


Portfolio Components:
- Table of Contents
- Personal Philosophy of Education
- What strengths do I bring that will help me become a teacher?
- What are my areas of growth?
- Why does being a teacher matter?
- What do I believe are the central tenants of good teaching? What do  good teachers do?
- What subjects or grade levels do I want to consider teaching?
- Video evidence from at least two sample teaching experiences within the class
- Written reflection on the practice teaching experiences (Number 3)
- Behavior Management Plan from Unit 2
- Written reflections including:
- Mentee Questions
- Steps to Becoming a Teacher Chart and Potential Plan
- Three classroom artifacts (from Instructional Practices) that best represent your work (summative assessments, analysis, classwork, etc.)
- Your goals for considering and pursuing an education profession
