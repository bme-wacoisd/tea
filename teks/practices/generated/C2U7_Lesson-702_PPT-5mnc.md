## Slide 5

### Benefits of Instructional Outcomes

- For Students
- Communicates what students need to know and be able to do
- Provides criteria for success in instructional tasks
- Helps to ensure that students are focusing on the most important content
- For Teachers
- Helps to make sure activities and practice are aligned to learning outcomes
- Ensure that all students are challenged by learning outcomes

## Slide 6

### 4 Ways Teachers Make Instructional Outcomes Clear for Students

- Making the instructional outcomes visible for students
- Reviewing instructional outcomes with students at the beginning of the lesson
- Returning to the instructional outcomes throughout the lesson as a formative check
- Reviewing the instructional outcomes at the conclusion of the lesson

## Slide 7

### I Can Statements

- Characteristics of Quality “I can” statements
- Use student-friendly language
- Focus on one key skill or concept
- Are measurable
- Process for Writing “I can” statements
- Start with the standard: Determine what instructional standard you will focus on during the lesson.
- Break the standard apart in to smaller, measurable skills. Try to keep one skill per “I can” statement.
- Describe each skill in a statement beginning with the phrase “I can”.  Check to make sure the statement uses language appropriate for the level of students.
