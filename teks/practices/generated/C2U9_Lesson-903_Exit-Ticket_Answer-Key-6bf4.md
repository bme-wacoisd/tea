### Lesson 9.03:  Practice-Formal, Written Feedback

Below are some written feedback statements that have been provided to students.  Examine each of the statements below and determine if the feedback statement meets the characteristics of effective feedback.  If so, explain why.  If not, make suggestions on how to revise the statement to better align to the characteristics of effective feedback.







| Feedback Statements | Effective Feedback   (Yes or No) | Explain Why /Make Suggestions to Improve |
| --- | --- | --- |
| “Great job providing specific sensory details in these sentences.” | Yes | The feedback is specific and clear.  It highlights strengths that can be replicated by the student. |
| “Consider improving your word choice.” | No | The feedback says to improve word choice but doesn’t explain how.  This statement could be revised to include specific details on what improving word choice means. |
| “I love your drawing.  You are a good artist.” | No | The feedback is focused on the student not the task.  The feedback should eliminate the good artist part and provide specific details on what was good about the drawing as related to the success criteria. |
| “Be sure to show all the steps of your thinking when working a problem like this.” | Yes | The feedback is related to the task and is specific and clear, providing the student specific information on how to improve. |
