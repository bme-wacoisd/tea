## Slide 4

### Quantitative Data vs. Qualitative Data

- Quantitative data are data that can be counted and expressed in numbers.  Quantitative data answer questions like “how many?” and “how often?”
- Qualitative data are data that are expressed in words. Qualitative data answers questions like “why?” and “how?”

## Slide 6

### Collecting Quantitative Data & Qualitative Data    to Develop a Learner Profile

- Have informal conversations and ask questions
- Access grades and other academic data
- Talk to student’s previous teachers, coaches, club sponsors, etc.
- Do classroom ice-breaker activities
- Review student records
- Give assignments that ask students to share more about themselves
- Send home a survey for students’ families to complete
- Administer a student survey
- Conduct home visits and family interviews
- Maintain a family communication tracking log

- Interest Inventories
- Communication Logs

- Formative Assessments
