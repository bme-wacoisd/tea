- Explain the steps of the Plan-Do-Study-Act continuous improvement cycle.
- Plan: Identify the problem, List possible steps to approach the problem, List desired outcomes
- Do: Apply the steps listed in the plan, Take notes/make observations during this time
- Study: Study the results after applying the plan. Did you see the outcome you expected? What were the actual results when compared to the desired ones?
- Act	Consider your next steps. Has the solution addressed the problem? Do you need to return to any of the previous step?

- How do continuous improvement cycles benefit teachers? How do they benefit students?
Continuous improvement cycles provide teachers with opportunities to plan, act, and reflect on problems that they may want to address in their classroom. This benefits the teacher because they can consistently grow into better, more confident, and more effective educators. That benefits students because the teacher is more aware of how effective their instruction is  and how students are learning, which may help teachers in identifying areas where students are struggling to be successful.
