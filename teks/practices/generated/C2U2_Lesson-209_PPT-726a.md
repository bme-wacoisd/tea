## Slide 7

### Success Criteria

- Motivate students: Communicate your belief in students’ potential and connect the daily work of the classroom with their goals and dreams.
- Maintains Economy of Language: Using fewer words helps students to focus on what they need to do to meet expectations and brings everyone back to the lesson as quickly as possible.
- Maintains Emotional Constancy: Use a neutral tone when delivering redirections of any kind.

## Slide 8

### How to Use Talk Aspirations


| Type | Example |
| --- | --- |
| To encourage students to sustain effort they are demonstrating | “Authors like us, who are learning to craft intriguing openings...” |
| To inspire them to tackle a challenge | “You’re Usain Bolt. Make this sprint your fastest yet.” |
| To connect the seemingly mundane moments to the big picture of where students are heading and who they’re becoming | “Just like an architect, try to do this square footage calculation in your head.” |
