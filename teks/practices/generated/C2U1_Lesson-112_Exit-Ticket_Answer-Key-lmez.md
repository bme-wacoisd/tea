Review the sample double-plan for the practice of a routine below. Complete the “Potential Pitfalls” and “How to Practice” columns of the table below.


| Step # | Teacher will. . . | Students will. . . | Potential Pitfalls | How to Practice |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Say, “Pause there. Tent your books. Question #___.” | Tent their books Take out their written response packets Turn to the appropriate question # Clear their desks of their folders. Pick up their pencils. | Students may not know what it means to “tent” their books. Students’ desks may become disorganized without a clear workspace. | Model “tenting” a book. Practice tenting and moving the book to the corner of the desk, taking out the packet and moving the folder off their desk. |
| 2 | Cold call a student to read the question. | Be ready to read the question when called on. Follow along silently. | Students may get distracted while clearing their desks. | Practice clearing desk and immediately turning to the question number, picking up their pencil, and pointing their pencil at the appropriate question. |
| 3 | Say, “Please get to work.” Set the timer and display it in a place where students can see it. | Pick up their pencils, open their books, and begin working. | Students may not get to work right away. | Practice “catching” them doing it right. Practice reminders and corrections. |
