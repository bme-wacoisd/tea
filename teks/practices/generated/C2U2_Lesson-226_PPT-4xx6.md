## Slide 4

### Vocabulary


| Positive Reinforcement | Feedback to students that follows a desired behavior and increases the likelihood that the behavior will be repeated. |
| --- | --- |
| Behavior Management System | A reinforcement-based system that acknowledges and rewards students for positive behaviors. |
| Classroom Reward System | A system where students earn tangible rewards by exhibiting targeted behaviors. |
| Targeted Behavior | The discrete behaviors that are explicitly taught and reinforced. |

- 4

---

## Slide 5

### Vocabulary


| Tangible rewards | Items such as stickers, dollars, toys, etc. that students can earn for exhibiting targeted behaviors. |
| --- | --- |
| Intrinsic Motivation | Behavior is driven by internal rewards, such as pleasurable feelings, personal satisfaction, enjoyment, etc.  You don’t expect anything in return. |
| Extrinsic Motivation | Behavior is driven by external or tangible rewards, such as toys, food, etc. or to avoid punishment |

- 5

## Slide 6

### Benefits of Classroom Reward Systems

- Positive behavior is reinforced
- Promotes positive and appropriate behavior
- Increased motivation
- Can provide a way for parents to track student behavior
- 6

---

## Slide 7

### Drawbacks of Classroom Reward Systems

- Focuses on extrinsic motivation – behavior is driven by external rewards, such as toys, food, etc., opposed to intrinsic motivation - behavior that is driven by internal rewards, pleasurable feelings, enjoyment, etc.
- Lacks specific feedback
- Can be difficult to manage over time
- Students become dependent on the reward
- Rewards become an expectation and lose their effect
- Increased pressure
- Potentially time-consuming for teachers (can detract from learning)
