## Slide 4

### Vocabulary

- Sequencing: The effective ordering of academic content to support students' understanding of a topic; sequencing also relies on building on prior knowledge to ensure that there are not gaps in student learning.
- Scaffolding: Structured supports offered to students by the teacher to help students attain a strong command of a new skill and/or concept
- Double Planning: Instructional strategy where teachers plan what students will be doing each step of the way to intentionally leverage opportunities for student learning and engagement.

## Slide 5

### Structuring Lessons

- The high leverage teaching strategy of Structuring Lessons brings together the concepts of:
- Classroom management and Sequencing of lessons
- 3 Key Considerations for Implementing HQIM with structure and intentionality:
- Clarity of Student Expectations: Begin with overview/review prior content.  This helps to activate student thinking and make connections between past learning and current learning as well as clarify expectations and student success criteria.
- Effective Transitions: Teachers must maximize the time available for learning.  This includes ensuring that transitions between activities are smooth and the teacher structures instructional activities to maximize time on task.
- Student Engagement: Students should be actively thinking, reading, writing, and talking about their learning throughout the lesson.
