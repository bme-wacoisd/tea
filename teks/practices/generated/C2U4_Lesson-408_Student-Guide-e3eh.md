Bellringer: Read the two scenarios. Determine which would lead to better learning and explain why.
Scenario #1: Mrs. James is teaching students about the changing shape of land. During her lesson, Mrs. James presents images of various landforms and has students consider what caused the formation through a series of open-ended questions. Students explore water and land interactions by using a model to investigate what happens to land when it interacts with waves along a shoreline.  Students analyze and compare solutions to problems caused by water changing the shape of the land. Students apply what they have learned using the engineering design process to develop a shoreline protection system that could slow changes to the shore of Montauk Point to prevent the Montauk Point Lighthouse from falling into the ocean.
Scenario #2: Mrs. Emery is also teaching students about the changing shape of land. She presents a PowerPoint on the role water plays in shaping land and creating landforms. Mrs. Emery shows students images of landforms and reads various texts about how different landforms were formed by water. At the end of the lesson, students take an assessment on water and land interactions.
