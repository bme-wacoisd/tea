### Lesson 5.10: Exploring Other Formative Assessment Options

Review each scenario below. On your own, determine the best assessment choice for each scenario. Explain your reasoning.



| Scenario | Assessment Choice | Reasoning |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Ms. Lopez is reviewing for a test in her math class. As a part of her review, she wants to know if they can identify cand analyze common misconceptions and mistakes. She wants to know if they can point out the mistake, explain the mistake and why it may have happened, and provide multiple options for fixing the mistake. Since they are about to take a test, she’d also like to give them the opportunity to learn from the suggestions of their peers. What type of assessment should she use? |  |  |
| Ms. Kelly is beginning an ELA unit on identity. During the unit, they are going to read and analyze several texts about individual and group identity, and they are going to write personal memoirs about the development of their own identity. Throughout the unit, Ms. Kelly would like students to know if students are able to identify and analyze themes of identify in each of the texts, consider how the author’s developed their texts. She’d also like student to prepare for their memoirs by reflecting on how each text they read connects with their own experiences to prepare for writing their memoirs. What type of formative assessment should Ms. Kelly use? |  |  |
| Mr. Patel is working through a unit on types of governments. At the end of the unit, students will need to be able to compare 5 common governments around the world. Mr. Patel has introduced the first two: socialism and communism. He knows that these can cause a bit of trouble for students so before he introduces another form of government, he wants to see if they can compare these two. What type of formative assessment should Mr. Patel use? |  |  |
