Fill in the blanks in the following statements about effective practice of roll-out routines.
- Scanning is when a teacher examines the room point by point by moving their eyes.
- When teachers model and describe routines, students see an example of what they are supposed to do.
- When teachers pre-empt pitfalls, they prepare for possible challenges and have a plan in place to address them during practice.
- When teachers isolate each step, students learn one step at a time.
- When teachers scan and give feedback, they reinforce the routine by helping students improve their understanding and actions.
Answer the following question.
What is the effect of deliberate student practice of routines?
Teachers anticipate student confusion, and students practice strategically simplified steps that prevent the confusion from taking hold. As a result, they are more likely to succeed in completing the routine.
