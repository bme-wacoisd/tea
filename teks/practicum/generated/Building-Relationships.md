# Building-Relationships


## Field-Based Experience: Practicum Teacher

### Tool Name: Building Relationships

Name:
Date:
Objective:
- Identify how strong relationships in the classroom build trust and rapport between students and teachers.
- Practice implementing an activity for building relationships in the classroom.
Purpose:  Strong relationships in the classroom build trust and rapport between students and teachers. The purpose of this tool is for students to observe and identify techniques for building relationships in the classroom.
Summary:  The tool requires students to:
- record their observations of techniques for building relationships in the classroom.
- interview a teacher about relationship-building techniques.
- implement and collect feedback on a relationship building activity.
- reflect upon the importance of intentionally building classroom relationships.
Connections to Course II:
- Lesson 2.02:  Building Trusting Relationships
- Lesson 2.03:  Developing Positive Relationships
Connected Instructional Practices TEKS:

- Standard 7: The student creates an effective learning environment. The student is expected to: (A) describe and implement a safe and an effective learning environment that incorporates the principles of universal design; (C) demonstrate teacher and trainer characteristics that promote an effective learning environment; (E) identify classroom-management techniques that promote an effective learning environment.
Connected Practicum TEKS:
- Standard 3:  The student explores the teaching and training field and profession. The student is expected to: (B) analyze practices of effective teaching and training professionals; (C) analyze qualities of effective schools.
- Standard 13:  The student continues to participate in field-based experiences in education or training. The student is expected to: (A) apply instructional strategies and concepts within a local educational or training facility; (B) document, assess, and reflect on instructional experiences.
