# Leveraging-School-Community


## Field-Based Experience: Practicum Teacher

### Tool Name: Leveraging the School Community

Name:
Date:
Objectives:
Explain the importance of coaches, club sponsors, and other school personnel in building relationships with students to support learning.
Understand how to use information about students from colleagues to support instructional decisions.
Purpose:  The purpose of this tool is for students to learn about how school communities provide multiple opportunities to learn about individual student’s interests and needs. This knowledge can be utilized to support students in the academic classroom.
Summary:  The tool provides a section for students to record their observations of the various roles and responsibilities of school community members. It also provides conversation starters for students to use with their field site campus teacher. Lastly, students meet with other members of the school community to learn more about an individual student, then discuss with the field site campus teacher the instructional implications of this collective knowledge.
Connections to Instructional Practices:
- Lesson 3.08:  Leveraging School Community to Support Instruction
Connected Instructional Practices TEKS:
- Standard 4: The student understands the learner and the learning process. The student is expected to: (A) relate and implement principles and theories of human development to teaching and training situations; (B) relate and implement principles and theories about the learning process to teaching and training situations; (C) demonstrate and implement behaviors and skills that facilitate learning.
- Standard 5:  The student interacts effectively in the role of an educator. The student is expected to: (A) demonstrate effective interaction skills with stakeholders such as students, educators, parents/guardians, community members, and other professionals.
Connected Practicum TEKS:
- Standard 13:  The student continues to participate in field-based experiences in education or training. The student is expected to: (A) apply instructional strategies and concepts within a local educational or training facility; (B) document, assess, and reflect on instructional experiences.
