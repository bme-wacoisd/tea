# Aligned-Assessment-3mr8


## Field-Based Experience: Practicum Teacher

### Tool Name: Aligned Assessment

Name:
Date:
Objectives:
- Develop and implement multiple forms of assessment within a lesson.
- Reflect upon the importance and impact of alignment between assessments and lesson objectives.
Purpose: The purpose of this tool is for students to validate the assessments they have embedded within their ongoing unit plan with best practices; students also reflect upon the consequences of objectives being misaligned with assessments.
Summary: This tool does not include an observation component; however, it directs the student to revisit the following tools if a refresher is needed on assessments: 5.1 Types of Assessment, 5.2 Characteristics of Effective Assessment, and 5.3 Best Practices for Assessment. Students are asked to revise and refine the assessments they have developed during their ongoing unit planning, and to seek feedback from the field site campus teacher, if appropriate. Lastly, students are invited to reflect on the consequences of assessments being misaligned with objectives from both the student and teacher perspectives.
Connections to Instructional Practices:
Lesson 6.11: Aligned Assessment

- Standard 6: The student plans and develops effective instruction. The student is expected to: (A) explain the role of the Texas Essential Knowledge and Skills in planning and evaluating instruction; (C) explain the rationale for and process of instructional planning components such as vertical alignment and scope and sequence; and (F) demonstrate lesson planning to meet instructional goals.
Connected Practicum TEKS:
- Standard 6:  The student plans and uses effective instruction. The student is expected to: (A) apply principles and theories that impact instructional planning.
- Standard 13:  The student continues to participate in field-based experiences in education or training. The student is expected to: (A) apply instructional strategies and concepts within a local educational or training facility; (B) document, assess, and reflect on instructional experiences.
