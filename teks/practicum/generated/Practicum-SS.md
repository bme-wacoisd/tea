| Course Three Tools | Course Three Tools |
| --- | --- |
| Tool Name | Purpose |
| Building Connections | The purpose of this tool is to provide students with the opportunity to observe how the implementation and modeling of systems and routines for entering the classroom, beginning class, turning in work, getting the attention of the class, transitions, and participating in discussions contribute to a safe and effective classroom environment. |
| Types of Routines | The purpose of this tool is to provide students with the opportunity to observe how systems and routines contribute to the creation of a joyful and focused classroom. |
| Effective Directions | The purpose of this tool is to provide students with the opportunity to observe how the planning and delivery of effective directions contribute to the creation of a joyful and focused classroom. |
| Transfer Ownership | The purpose of this tool is to provide students with the opportunity to observe how the planning and implementation of System Cues can create an environment in which students are empowered to execute directions with a high degree of independence, thereby allowing the teacher to focus their energy on facilitating learning. |
| Do It Again | The purpose of this tool is to provide students with the opportunity to observe how the Do It Again technique can be used to pause students in the midst of any sub-par execution of a task and giving them the chance to do it again, better, setting the standard for quality in the class community, while reinforcing classroom systems, routines, and culture. |
| Building Relationships | The purpose of this tool is to provide students with the opportunity to identify strategies and techniques for building relationships between students and with the teacher to develop trust and rapport and establish consistency and high expectations. |
| Strong Voice | The purpose of this tool is to provide students with the opportunity to observe strong voice as a technique for adjusting speech and body language with intentionality to maximize student attention, ensure that students listen carefully, and to signal that what is being said is important. |
| Positive Framing | The purpose of this tool is to provide students with the opportunity to identify the key components of Positive Framing as a way to frame interactions, particularly corrections, in a way that reinforces trust, while still guiding students toward a better course of action. |
| Challenge & Talk Aspirations | The purpose of this tool is for students to learn how to Challenge students and use Talk Aspirations to motivate and inspire them to sustain or improve their performance. |
| Classroom Rules | The purpose of this tool is to provide students with the opportunity to observe the characteristics of effective classroom rules and the impact on instruction. |
| Classroom Layout | The purpose of this tool is to provide students with the opportunity to identify the components of a well-designed classroom: safe and effective learning environment, smooth transitions, whole group discussion, small group work, and access to materials. |
| Behavior Management | The purpose of this tool is to provide students with the opportunity to identify and apply effective behavior management techniques. |
| Classroom Procedures | The purpose of this tool is to provide students with the opportunity to identify the key components of effective classroom management. |
| Authentic Listening | The purpose of this tool is to provide students with the opportunity to identify the key components of authentic listening to build trust and rapport with students in a way that can lead to a positive impact on student learning. |
| Trust Generating Practices | The purpose of this tool is to provide students with the opportunity to establish and maintain boundaries while using trust generators to build rapport with students in order to create a safe and effective classroom. |
| Asset-Based Approaches | The purpose of this tool is to provide students with the opportunity to observe how an asset-based approach to teaching leverages students’ strengths while maintaining, communicating, and supporting all students in achieving high expectations for behavioral and academic achievement. |
| Funds of Knowledge | The purpose of this tool is to provide students with the opportunity to observe how teachers can understand and use students’ funds of knowledge to connect the classroom to students lived experiences and enhance an asset-based view of students. |
| Leveraging School Community | The purpose of this tool is to provide students with the opportunity to learn more about school communities, how they provide multiple and invaluable resources for learning more about individual students, their interests, and their needs, and how this collective knowledge can be utilized to support students in the academic classroom. |
| Leveraging Families | The purpose of this tool is to provide students with the opportunity to observe the importance of frequent and consistent communication with students’ families and caregivers to forge partnerships and leverage their expertise in challenging situations, as well as in celebrating successes. |
| Information About Students | The purpose of this tool is to provide students with the opportunity to observe the importance of students’ funds of knowledge in making instructional decisions. |
| Quantitative & Qualitative Data | The purpose of this tool is to provide students with the opportunity to observe how the collection and compilation of quantitative and qualitative data about students, perhaps with learner profiles, can be used to learn about them as individuals, develop positive relationships, and to make instructional decisions that support student learning. |
| Simple Memory Model | The purpose of this tool is to provide students with the opportunity to observe how understanding and applying the simple memory model and related practices can support effective instruction. |
| Science of Learning | The purpose of this tool is to provide students with the opportunity to observe how knowledge of the science of learning principles provide a framework to guide teaching and instructional decision-making. |
| Attending to the Most Important Content | The purpose of this tool is to provide students with the opportunity to observe how the focus of students’ attention in class will determine the level of engagement of their working memory and the likelihood of ensuring lasting learning. |
| Prior Knowledge | The purpose of this tool is to provide students with the opportunity to observe the importance of prior knowledge in new learning. |
| Sequencing & Scaffolding | The purpose of this tool is to provide students with the opportunity to observe the importance of sequencing and scaffolding as instructional strategies for avoiding cognitive overload and supporting students’ understanding of content. |
| Cognitive Engagement | The purpose of this tool is to provide students with the opportunity to observe the importance and impact of cognitive engagement on learning. |
| Rigorous Tasks | The purpose of this tool is to provide students with the opportunity to observe how rigorous tasks cognitively engage students. |
| Examples & Non-Examples | The purpose of this tool is to provide students with the opportunity to observe how examples and nonexamples in a lesson help students recognize a concept or idea in a variety of contexts and develop and accurate mental picture of what the concept is and is not. |
| Meaningful Questioning | The purpose of this tool is to provide students with the opportunity to observe how the TLAC Stretch It technique can be used to create meaningful questions that lead to cognitive engagement. |
| Practice with Feedback | The purpose of this tool is to provide students with the opportunity to observe how practice with feedback can engage students’ working memory and move ideas and content to long-term. |
| Defining Assessment | The purpose of this tool is to provide students with the opportunity to observe how formative and summative assessments are used to diagnose and evaluate student learning. |
| Characteristics of Effective Assessment | The purpose of this tool is to provide students with the opportunity to identify the key components of effective assessments: alignment with learning targets, fairness and transparency, appropriate level of rigor, appropriate content prioritization, practical, cognitively engaging. |
| Best Practices for Assessment | The purpose of this tool is to provide students with a mental map to think about the ecosystem of informal and formal assessments and their impact on classroom pacing, grouping, remediation, and enrichment. |
| Formative Assessment Strategies | This tool provides students the opportunity to observe the use of informal formative assessments and consider various implementation strategies and their impact. |
| Strategic Questioning | This tool synthesizes the use of the Wiederhold Question Matrix as an observation device, provides additional experience with composing effective questions, and offers students the opportunity to reflect upon effective question delivery criteria. |
| The Exit Ticket | This tool provides students with an opportunity to analyze exit tickets from previous lessons for success criteria (relevance, brevity, and variety), practice their own construction of exit tickets, and reflect upon students’ perceptions of exit tickets. |
| Other Formative Assessments | The purpose of this tool is to align connections between various formative assessments and student learning experiences using field notes, practice, and reflection. |
| Summative Assessments | This tool synthesizes student understanding of various summative assessments by evaluating assessment effectiveness, designing a performance task, and imagining the components of a large-scale portfolio assessment. |
| Rubrics | This tool provides students the opportunity to make field notes about rubric quality, design a rubric and/or calibrate rubric scoring, and create a list of unreliable, but commonly used rubric descriptions. |
| High Quality Curricula | This tool assists students in refining their understanding of the relationships among curricula, units, and lessons along with relationships among standards, objectives, and assessments in consideration of high-quality instructional materials. |
| Unit Plans | The purpose of this tool is to enhance student understanding of unit plan sequencing through visual decomposition of a previous unit plan, development of a future unit plan, and reflection upon how unit sequencing impacts students and teachers. |
| Lesson Plan Design | The purpose of this tool is to encourage dialogue between the student and the field site campus teacher about lesson planning, to provide guidance on planning lessons for the unit plan that was initiated in the previous tool (6.2 Unit Plans), and to promote reflection on the relationship between high-quality lesson plans and student learning experiences. |
| Lesson Sequencing | This tool provides students the opportunity to observe the sequencing of lesson complexity, to evaluate their previously designed lessons through the lens of sequencing, and to reflect upon the impact of sequencing on student learning. |
| Lesson Scaffolding | This tool provides students the opportunity to plot lesson activities on a Gradual Release of Responsibility graphic, to develop and perform a modeling activity, and to reflect on components of both effective and ineffective student practice. |
| Aligned Assessment | The purpose of this tool is to give students the opportunity to validate the assessments they have embedded within their ongoing unit plan with best practices; students also reflect upon the consequences of objectives being misaligned with assessments. |
| High Leverage Teaching Practices | The purpose of this tool is to refamiliarize students with the seven high-leverage teaching practices they learned in Course II and allow them to investigate additional emerging research, as well as to consider the importance of staying abreast of evidence-based strategies. |
| UDL – Action and Expression | The purpose of this tool is for students to observe the Universal Design for Learning Guideline of providing multiple means of action and expression to support learning for all students. |
| UDL – Engagement | The purpose of this tool is for students to observe the Universal Design for Learning Guideline of providing multiple means of engagement to support learning for all students. |
| UDL – Representation | The purpose of this tool is for students to observe the Universal Design for Learning Guideline of providing multiple means of representation to support learning for all students. |
