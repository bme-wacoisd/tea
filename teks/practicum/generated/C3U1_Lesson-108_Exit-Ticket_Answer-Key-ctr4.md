What are non-traditional educators?
Non-traditional educators are professionals who develop and implement programs intended to improve employees’ skills and knowledge in an industry.
Darryl recently graduated from college with a double major in education and business. He is looking for a job teaching adults for an organization in the business field. What job titles should he be looking for in his search?
Corporate training specialist; development manager; education and development manager; Learning and development specialist; training coordinator.

- Explain how the responsibilities of non-traditional educators differ from that of K-12 educators.
Non-traditional educators teach content directly relevant to the job that their learners are doing. They also determine what to teach based on need instead of based on set standards that all employees must learn. Most importantly, their audience is different—they teach adults and not children. This means they cannot talk to them like children and must treat them like professionals.
