## Slide 4

### Vocabulary


| Term | Definition |
| --- | --- |
| Phonics | The study of the relationships between letters and the sounds they represent |
| Systematic Phonics Instruction | Structured phonics instructional routines that provide step-by-step procedures and a scope and sequence of skills from less complex to more complex to allow students to build upon prior knowledge |
| Explicit Phonics Instruction | Teacher directed phonics instruction that clearly explains and models each individual concept |
| Incidental Phonics | Teacher directed instruction that does not follow a planned sequence of phonics concepts to guide instruction. Concepts and skills are taught as they appear in text. |

## Slide 7

### Systematic VS Incidental


| Systematic | Incidental |
| --- | --- |
| Sound/spelling relationships are directly taught in isolation | Sound and spelling relationships are inferred from reading whole words |
| Direct instruction and practice with blending previously taught sound/spelling patterns | Sound and spelling relationships are introduced as they are encountered in texts |
| Sound/spelling relationships are introduced in a logical sequence and increase in difficulty | No logical sequence or progression of skills |
