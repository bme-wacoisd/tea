### Lesson 1.11: Personal Philosophy of Education

Respond to the prompts below:
- What are key components of a teacher's educational philosophy?
Teacher beliefs about how students learn and how they want to teach.
Examples of how their beliefs might show up in their teaching.

- Why would a teacher want to update their educational philosophy?
- As a teacher your beliefs change over time, and you would want them to be a part of your educational philosophy.
- This is something you would share with a potential employer. You want to be accurate and highlight what you have learned about teaching.

- How does a teacher-centered philosophy differ from that of a student-centered philosophy?
Teacher-centered philosophies focus on having the teacher be the one who is charge in the classroom and making all the decisions. A student-centered philosophy promotes student ownership of learning, and students get to make decision about how they learn.
Teachers are the only decision maker in the classroom with teacher-centered philosophy, and decision making is shared in the student-centered philosophy.
