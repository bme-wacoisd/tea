## Slide 4

### Vocabulary


| Burnout | A state of chronic stress that leads to physical and emotional exhaustion, cynicism, detachment, and feelings of ineffectiveness and lack of accomplishment. |
| --- | --- |
| Empathy Fatigue | A condition where an individual experiences emotional and physical exhaustion, leading to a decreased ability to empathize or feel compassion for others. |

## Slide 5

### Categories of Teacher Stress

- School Organizations that lack strong principal leadership, a healthy school climate and a collegial, supportive environment.
- Job Demands that are escalating with high-stakes testing, student behavioral problems, and difficult parents.
- Work Resources that limit a teacher’s sense of autonomy and decision-making power.
- Teacher Social and Emotional Competence to manage stress and nurture a healthy classroom.

## Slide 8

### Homework: Teacher Interview

- Directions: Use the questions below to guide an interview about stress and burnout with a current teacher.
- What do you find most stressful about being a teacher
- What was something about teaching you used to find very stressful that you no longer find stressful? Why did it change?
- What are your strategies for stress management?
- Has there been a time period while you’ve been teaching that you felt burnt out? How did you overcome that?
- If you had to give advice to a new teacher about stress management, what would you tell them?

### Exit Ticket

- Which of the following is not a warning sign of burning out?
- Exhaustion, fatigue, and difficulty sleeping.
- Feeling depressed and withdrawn from others.
- Frustrated after a class behaves poorly.
- Physical symptoms like headaches, stomachaches, racing heart.
- How do stressors contribute to the higher rate of teacher burnout? Why is it important to manage the stressors that contribute to teacher burnout?
