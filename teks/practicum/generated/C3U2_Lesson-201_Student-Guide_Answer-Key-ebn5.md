Professionalism & Ethical Behavior

Key Areas of Teacher Professionalism

Why do you think it is important for educators to exhibit professionalism and ethical behavior?

Teachers are in a position of public trust and must demonstrate integrity, honesty, and good moral character. When you exhibit professionalism, it shows that you take your role seriously and care about your students and the dignity of the teaching profession. Professionalism communicates to parents, students, colleagues, and administration that you can be trusted.

The Educator’s Code of Ethics lists the state standards for ethical behavior. Professional behavior is also described and enforced at the district level. Districts use an Employee Standards of Conduct to outline the professional expectations for employees.

Why are ethical standards and guidelines necessary?
The guidelines help protect the safety and welfare of Texas schoolchildren and school personnel and helps ensure educators and applicants are fit to instruct or to supervise the youth of the state.
