| 3 mins | Bellringer: Today you will present your professional development plan to a group of peers. What are three things that you think presenters should keep in mind today about making an effective presentation? | Student Guide |
| 3 mins | T: What are some things that you identified to keep in mind as a presenter? S: (Student responses will vary.)  T: (Teacher captures a list of the responses on the board.)  T: Let’s briefly compare this list we generated to the success criteria for presentations.   T: (Share via PPT.) Success Criteria for Presentations Student uses relevant facts and details to support key ideas. Student makes eye contact with audience throughout the presentation. Student speaks clearly with appropriate tone and volume to keep audience engaged. Student uses appropriate language and vocabulary.  Student answers questions from audience clearly.   T: What similarities do you see? Did we miss anything?   S: (Responses will vary.)  T: Please refer to this list as you present your project to peers today. | PPT |
| 8 mins | Introduce Student Presentations   T: In a moment, I will divide you into groups of four to conduct your presentations on your professional development plan.  Each group member will have 5 minutes to present their professional growth goals, their plan to attain those goals, and how they will track their progress toward those goals over the course of the year. I will keep time and will give a 1-minute warning before it is time to move to the next presenter.   T: There is a Student Observation Form in today’s student guide. Please use this form to provide feedback to your peers on their presentations. You will fill out one form for each presenter. Please remember to adhere to the Tips for Giving and Receiving Effective Feedback that are included in your student guide for reference. Please take a moment to review the tips and identify 3 things you want to keep in mind while giving feedback today.   S: (Review Tips for Giving and Receiving Effective Feedback.)  T: Who can share something we should keep in mind while giving and receiving feedback today. (Call on student volunteers.)  S: (Responses will vary but should align with items listed in the Tips for Giving and Receiving Effective Feedback.)  T: Are there any questions before we begin?   S: (Ask clarifying questions.)  T: (Address student questions as needed.)  T: (Divide students into groups of 4 for final presentations.) As you are presenting, I will keep a timer to ensure that everyone gets time to present.  I will provide you a one-minute warning before it is time to switch to the next presenter.  You may move to your group now and identify the order in which you will present.  S: (Move to identified groups and identify order of presentations.)  T: First presenters raise your hand. Great!  How about the second presenters? Great.  What about those who will be presenting third? Great.  And who will present fourth?  Excellent.  Remember this order so you can make sure you transition quickly between each presenter.  We will get started now.  First presenters you may begin.  (Set timer for 5 minutes, providing a 1-minute reminder before the end of the time.)  S: (Begin group presentations.)  T: (Circulate to listen to presentations and provide support as needed.) | Student Guide |
