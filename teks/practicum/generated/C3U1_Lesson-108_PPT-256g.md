## Slide 4

### Vocabulary

- Non-traditional Educators: professionals who develop and implement programs intended to improve employees’ skills and knowledge in an industry outside of K-12 schools.
- Andragogy: the method and practice of teaching adult learners
- 4

## Slide 5

### Job Titles

- Corporate Training Specialist
- Development manager
- Education and development manager
- Learning and development specialist
- Training coordinator
- 5

---

## Slide 6

### Responsibilities

- Developing and leading training sessions for new or existing employees
- Adapting training sessions to support learner needs
- Assessing training needs of organization
- Communicating training needs to management
- Reviewing team performance and progress
- Providing coaching and feedback to employees
- 6

---

## Slide 7

### Required Education/Certification

- Require a bachelor’s degree in one of the following:
- Education
- Human Resources Training and Development
- Business
- The most qualified candidates often have a master’s degree in curriculum and instruction.
- Additional certifications exist (but are not required)
- 7

---

## Slide 8

### Salary

- In 2020, a corporate trainer received median pay of $62,700 per year, according to the US Bureau of Labor Statistics.
- Experience and education level can lead to higher salary.
- 8

---

## Slide 9

### Non-traditional Educator Pathways

- Option 1:
- Earn a degree in Education or Human Resources Training and Development with the goal of becoming a corporate trainer immediately.
- Option 2:
- Earn a degree in a specific field, gain experience in that field, and then leverage that experience to find work in corporate training.
