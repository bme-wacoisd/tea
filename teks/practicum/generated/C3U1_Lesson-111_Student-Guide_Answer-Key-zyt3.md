### Lesson 1.11: Personal Philosophy of Education

Name:

Bellringer:
Reflect on why it might be important for a teacher to capture in writing their educational philosophy and to reflect on and revise it throughout their career.
Responses may vary but may include:
It requires a teacher to name what they believe about teaching. It makes them think about it and narrow it down.
- Sometimes what we believe or what we know might change, and it would be important to make sure that was a part of our philosophy so that our philosophies are accurate.


Notes: Components of the Education Philosophy



| Teacher-centered philosophy | Teachers’ belief that their main goal as a teacher is to transfer the knowledge and values that their students will need to do well whey that graduate and move into society. |
| --- | --- |
| Student-centered philosophy | Teachers’ belief that the student should be central in the learning and that the student actively shapes the learning environment and direction of the class. There are elevated levels of student ownership of learning. |
| Core beliefs | Our most deeply held assumptions about ourselves and the world around use. These are beliefs that drive our thinking and our behaviors. |
