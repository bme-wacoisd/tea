- How do students benefit from productive struggle?
- Students can better understand the concept or skill because they can practice in a low-stakes environment. The student can feel comfortable with being wrong during the process of productive struggle, and is able to practice, refine, and revise their understanding through use of multiple strategies.

- How do teachers benefit from allowing students to struggle productively?
- While students are working through their challenge, teachers can better see which students are struggling with conceptual understanding or procedural fluency. Additionally, the teacher can tell if there is a misconception that one or multiple students hold that they should address to the whole class.

- What challenges might a teacher experience when asking students to complete a task through productive struggle?
- Teachers may struggle to allow students to work through the challenge without intervening or giving too much help.
