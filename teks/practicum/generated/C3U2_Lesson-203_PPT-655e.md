## Slide 4

### Effective Written Communication

- 4
- What is effective communication?
- The process of exchanging ideas, knowledge, opinions, facts, or data in a clear and purposeful manner so that it is understood by all involved.
- Why must teachers communicate effectively?
- Because it helps create a safe and positive learning environment and to help students succeed academically and personally.
- Why must teachers have effective written communication skills?
- Teachers use written communication for a large majority of their work and use written communication to interact with multiple stakeholders in education. They must have them to be successful in their jobs.

---

## Slide 5

### Effective Written Communication

- 5
- Who is a teacher’s audience? Students, Parents and families, Other teachers or school professionals
- What are some examples of how teachers use written communication?
- Students: Directions and written assignments, feedback on student assignments, progress reports
- Parents and families: Newsletters or blogs, progress reports, email exchanges, IEPs
- Other teachers and school professionals: Emails, written communication in professional learning communities, updates on student progress for student intervention plans

---

## Slide 6

### Effective Written Communication

- 6
- What are the success criteria of effective written communication by teachers?
- Effective written communication includes:
- A goal clearly stated
- Is welcoming and has an appropriate tone for the audience (formality and age appropriate)
- Is simple and stays on topic
- Free from grammatical or spelling errors
- Professional content
