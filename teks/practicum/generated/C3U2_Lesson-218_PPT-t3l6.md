## Slide 4

### Vocabulary


| Continuing Education | Education courses and training for teachers to stay current with the latest developments in knowledge, theories, skills, and new technologies in the field of education. Continuing education is required to maintain a teaching license. |
| --- | --- |
| Continuing Education Credits | The measurement of hours of courses and training the teacher receives for continuing education. A certain number of credits are required to maintain/renew a teaching license. |

## Slide 5

### What is Continuing Professional Education (CPE)?

- CPE is the Texas equivalent to Continuing Education. It is required for all Texas educators holding a standard teaching certificate. The appropriate number of clock-hours of continuing professional education (CPE) must be completed during each five-year license renewal period.  Classroom teachers must complete 150 CPE hours.
- 5

---

## Slide 6

### How Do Teachers Receive CPE?

- Teachers can receive CPE hours in many ways, including through taking online courses, training sessions, participate in teacher mentoring, attending conferences or workshops, professional development opportunities by the school or the district. Organizations that provide CPE hours must be approved by the Texas Education Agency.

## Slide 7

### CPE and Teacher Success

- Teachers stay up to date on new research on how students learn
- Teachers stay up to date on emerging technology skills for the classroom
- Teachers interact/learn about new curriculum and resources
- Techers connect with other teachers to promote effective collaboration
- Teachers develop new skills and strategies
- Teachers can refine their existing skills

## Slide 8

### Where Do Teachers Get CPEs?

- School districts and open-enrollment charter schools
- TEA
- State Board for Educator Certification
- Accredited institutions of higher education recognized by the Texas Higher Education Coordinating Board (THECB). This includes out-of-state institutions of higher education recognized by the THECB.
- Regional education service centers (ESCs)
- Registered TEA Providers
