## Slide 4

### Vocabulary


| Active Listening | Way of listening and responding to another person that promotes mutual understanding by making a conscious effort to hear, understand, and retain information shared with you. |
| --- | --- |
| Conflict | A disagreement or argument |
| “I” Statements | Style of communication that focuses on the feelings or beliefs of the speaker. |

## Slide 5

### Active Listening During Conflict

- Making eye contact
- Facing the other person
- Refrain from interrupting
- Acknowledging the other person (nods, etc.)
- Repeat or restate what the speaker says
- 5

---

## Slide 6

### Nonverbal Communication in Conflict

- Relax your stance
- Show interest or care/concern through facial expressions
- Use a calm tone of voice to help de-escalate the conflict
- 6

---

## Slide 7

### Confront the Situation, Not the Person

- Don’t make it personal—address what is wrong with the situation, not the person.
- 7

---

## Slide 8

### Using “I” Statements

- Communicating using “I” to share your perspective without accusing/attacking others.
- 8
