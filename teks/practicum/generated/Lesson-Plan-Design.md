# Lesson-Plan-Design


## Field-Based Experience: Practicum Teacher

### Tool Name: Lesson Plan Design

Name:
Date:
Objectives:
- Observe and reflect on the components of a high-quality lesson plan.
- Explore and reflect on how a high-quality lesson plan leads to mastery of grade-level content.
- Rehearse and teach part or all of a lesson plan.
Purpose: The purpose of this tool is for students to learn about lesson planning, to rehearse and teach part or all of a lesson plan, and to promote reflection on the relationship between high-quality lesson plans and student learning experiences.
Summary: This tool invites the student to interview the field site campus teacher to learn more about their favorite lesson to teach and consider how the essential components of lesson planning facilitate teaching and learning. Next, students will rehearse and teach part or all of a lesson plan and discuss feedback with the field site teacher. Lastly, students are asked to reflect upon essential components and how students should interact with standards and objectives.
Connections to Instructional Practices:
Lesson 6.03: Components of a Lesson Plan
Lesson 6.04: Standard & Objective Alignment
Lesson 6.05: Components of a High-Quality Objective
Connected Instructional Practices TEKS:

- Standard 6: The student plans and develops effective instruction. The student is expected to: (A) explain the role of the Texas Essential Knowledge and Skills in planning and evaluating instruction; (C) explain the rationale for and process of instructional planning components such as vertical alignment and scope and sequence; and (F) demonstrate lesson planning to meet instructional goals.
Connected Practicum TEKS:
- Standard 6:  The student plans and uses effective instruction. The student is expected to: (A) apply principles and theories that impact instructional planning.
- Standard 13:  The student continues to participate in field-based experiences in education or training. The student is expected to: (A) apply instructional strategies and concepts within a local educational or training facility; (B) document, assess, and reflect on instructional experiences.
