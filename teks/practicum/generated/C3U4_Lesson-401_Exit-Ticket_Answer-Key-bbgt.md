Why is using research-based instructional strategies in math important to the academic achievement of all math students?
Research tells us that there are practices that can lead to better student outcomes.
Too often students quickly categorize themselves as students who are good in math or not good at math. When teachers use RBIS, they help all students to recognize that they are capable at math regardless of grade level.

Describe the difference between procedural fluency and conceptual understanding.
Procedural fluency focuses on helping students to complete calculations quickly and accurately. Conceptual understanding builds the ability to access concepts from a number of perspectives and to see math as more than a set of mnemonics or discrete procedures.
