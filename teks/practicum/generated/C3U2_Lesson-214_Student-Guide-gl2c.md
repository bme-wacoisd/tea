## Student Guide

### Lesson 2.14: What is Teacher Burnout?

Name:
Date:
Bellringer:
Work with a group or partner to brainstorm a list of sources of teacher stress.


Debrief




Notes: What is Burnout?




Video Viewing: What is Teacher Burnout?
Directions: You will watch a video called “Stress and Teacher Burnout” created by PBS. Answer the guided questions below.

1. What are some contextual factors that can cause stress to a teacher?


2. What is empathy distress or empathy fatigue in your own words? (Hint: Opposite of compassion and what too much empathy can do.)


3. What is short-term stress? What is long-term stress?


4. How does burnout happen? Does it happen right away?


5. Why are good teachers able to burnout?



Small Group Discussion: Teacher Burnout
Directions: Independently read the article, Teacher Burnout: Causes, Symptoms, and Prevention.
In small groups, using your notes, the article, and the teacher video, you will discuss the causes, symptoms and difficulties related to teacher burnout. The questions below are intended to be guiding questions for your conversation.
Norms:
Be respectful while others are speaking
Listen actively
Allow everyone the opportunity to speak
1. What is teacher burnout? How is it different than short-term stress?


2. What are some stressors or contextual factors that can cause burnout? Why do you think these would cause burnout over a long period of time? (Hint: Video)


3. What are the symptoms of burnout? Why is it important to recognize them?


4. Why should we prevent teacher burnout? How does teacher burnout affect students and the wider school?
